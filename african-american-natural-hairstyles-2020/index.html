Introduction

2020 was a year of change and self-discovery, especially for African American women who decided to embrace their natural hair. Natural hairstyles have become a symbol of empowerment, self-expression, and embracing one's cultural heritage. In this article, we will explore some popular African American natural hairstyles in 2020 and celebrate the beauty of natural hair.

The Big Chop: Bold and Liberating

The "big chop" is a hairstyle choice that involves cutting off all chemically processed or relaxed hair, leaving behind only the natural hair. This bold move is often seen as a liberating experience for African American women, as it allows them to embrace their natural texture and start a journey towards self-acceptance.

Protective Styles: A Blend of Elegance and Practicality

Protective styles like braids, twists, and wigs have been widely popular in the African American community in 2020. These styles not only offer versatility and a chance to experiment with different looks but also provide protection to the natural hair, reducing damage and promoting healthy growth.

Bantu Knots: A Stylish and Cultural Statement

Bantu knots are a traditional African hairstyle that has gained immense popularity in recent years. This style involves sectioning the hair and twisting it into small knots, resulting in a unique and eye-catching look. Bantu knots are not only a stylish choice but also a way to celebrate African culture and heritage.

Twist Outs: Defined Curls and Volume

Twist outs are a go-to hairstyle for African American women who want to enhance their natural curls and add volume. This style involves twisting small sections of damp hair and allowing them to dry before unraveling the twists, resulting in beautifully defined curls with a touch of elegance.

Afro Puffs: A Classic and Timeless Look

Afro puffs have been a beloved natural hairstyle for decades, and they continue to be a popular choice in 2020. This versatile style involves gathering the hair into a puff or two on top of the head, creating a chic and effortless look that can be worn casually or dressed up for special occasions.

Cornrows: A Traditional and Artistic Choice

Cornrows are a traditional African hairstyle that has been passed down through generations. In 2020, cornrows have made a comeback, with women embracing the intricate and artistic patterns that can be created with this style. Cornrows not only showcase creativity but also provide a low-maintenance option for those who prefer to keep their hair protected and tangle-free.

Tutorial: Achieving the Perfect Wash and Go

The wash and go is a popular natural hairstyle that is all about embracing the hair's natural texture and pattern. In this tutorial, we will guide you through the steps to achieve the perfect wash and go, from cleansing and conditioning to styling and defining your curls for a flawless and effortless look.

Product Review: Must-Have Natural Hair Products

In 2020, various natural hair products have emerged to cater specifically to the needs of African American women. In this section, we will review some must-have products, including moisturizers, leave-in conditioners, and styling gels, that can help you achieve and maintain healthy, vibrant, and well-defined natural hair.

Tips for Healthy Natural Hair: Nourish and Flourish

Keeping your natural hair healthy and strong is essential for achieving any desired hairstyle. In this section, we will provide you with tips and tricks to promote hair growth, prevent breakage, and maintain moisture, ensuring that your natural hair remains beautiful and resilient.

Conclusion

2020 was a transformative year for African American women, as they embraced their natural hair and celebrated their unique beauty. From the big chop to protective styles, the variety of natural hairstyles showcased the versatility and richness of African American culture. With the right products, care, and styling techniques, anyone can rock their natural hair and feel confident in their own skin.
