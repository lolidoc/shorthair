How Should A 60 Year Old Apply Makeup

Introduction

As we age, our skin goes through various changes, and so does our makeup routine. Applying makeup in your 60s requires a different approach to enhance your natural beauty while addressing specific concerns. This article will guide you on how to apply makeup that is suitable for a 60-year-old woman.

Skincare Preparation

Prior to applying makeup, it is essential to prepare your skin. Start by cleansing your face with a gentle cleanser to remove any impurities. Follow it up with a hydrating serum and a moisturizer specifically formulated for mature skin. This will provide a smooth canvas for your makeup application.

Foundation and Concealer

Choose a lightweight, moisturizing foundation that matches your skin tone. Avoid heavy formulas that can settle into fine lines and wrinkles. Apply foundation using a damp sponge or a brush, blending it evenly onto your face. Use a creamy concealer to cover any dark spots, blemishes, or under-eye circles.

Eye Makeup

For a natural look, opt for neutral shades such as taupe, brown, or soft pinks. Apply a light-colored eyeshadow on the lid and a slightly darker shade in the crease to add dimension. Use a waterproof mascara to define your lashes, focusing on the outer corners to lift your eyes.

Blush and Bronzer

Adding a touch of blush and bronzer can liven up your complexion. Choose a blush in a peachy or rosy shade and apply it to the apples of your cheeks. Sweep a matte bronzer along the hollows of your cheeks and temples to create a subtle contouring effect.

Lipstick and Lip Liner

Opt for creamy lipsticks or lip stains in shades that complement your skin tone. Avoid dark or matte shades that can make your lips appear thinner. Use a lip liner to define your lips and prevent feathering. Apply the lipstick and top it off with a lip gloss for added moisture and a youthful sheen.

Finishing Touches

Set your makeup with a lightweight translucent powder to reduce shine and increase longevity. Avoid using a heavy powder as it can settle into fine lines. Use a setting spray to lock your makeup in place and give your skin a dewy finish.

Eyebrows

As we age, our eyebrows can become sparse. Fill in any gaps using an eyebrow pencil or powder that matches your natural brow color. Use light, feathery strokes to mimic the appearance of natural hair.

Highlighter

A subtle touch of highlighter can add a youthful glow to your skin. Apply a small amount on the high points of your face, such as the cheekbones, brow bone, and the bridge of your nose. This will enhance your features and give your skin a radiant look.

Cleansing and Moisturizing

At the end of the day, ensure you thoroughly cleanse your face to remove all traces of makeup. Follow it up with a nourishing night cream to replenish your skin while you sleep. Consistent skincare routine will help maintain the health and vitality of your skin.

Conclusion

Applying makeup in your 60s is all about enhancing your natural beauty and addressing specific concerns. By following the tips mentioned in this article, you can achieve a youthful and radiant look that boosts your confidence. Remember, the key is to embrace your age gracefully and have fun with your makeup routine!
