Understanding the Science of Hair Growth

Hair growth is a complex process that occurs in cycles. Each hair follicle goes through a growth phase, a rest phase, and a shedding phase. The length and thickness of your hair are determined by various factors, including genetics, hormones, and overall health.

During the growth phase, new hair cells are produced in the hair follicle and push the existing hair shaft upward. This phase can last anywhere from two to seven years. After the growth phase, the hair follicle enters a rest phase, known as the telogen phase. During this phase, the hair follicle rests and no new hair growth occurs. Finally, the hair enters the shedding phase, where the old hair falls out and is replaced by a new hair shaft.
