Are you looking to recreate the iconic hairstyles of the 60s? Short hair was all the rage during this fashionable decade, and learning how to style short hair 60s can give you a retro and stylish look. In this blog post, we will explore different techniques and tips on how to achieve that classic 60s hairstyle.

The 60s was a time of bold and glamorous hairdos, and short hair was no exception. However, styling short hair can be challenging, especially if you're aiming for a specific era like the 60s. Many people struggle with finding the right techniques and products to achieve the desired look. But fear not, because we're here to help you master the art of styling short hair 60s.

So, how can you style short hair in a 60s-inspired way? The key is to focus on volume, texture, and the right accessories. By using the right tools and following the right steps, you can easily achieve a stunning 60s look that will turn heads everywhere you go.

To begin with, let's summarize the main points of how to style short hair 60s. First, you need to focus on creating volume and texture in your hair. This can be achieved through backcombing, using hot rollers, or teasing your hair. Second, accessories are essential in achieving an authentic 60s look. Think headbands, scarves, and hair clips. Lastly, don't forget to finish off your look with hairspray to ensure your hairstyle stays in place all day long.

How to Style Short Hair 60s: Step-by-Step Guide

Now that we've covered the basics, let's dive deeper into the step-by-step process of styling short hair in a 60s-inspired way. Follow these instructions to achieve the perfect 60s look:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that add volume and texture. Once your hair is dry, apply a volumizing mousse or spray to the roots.

Step 2: Backcomb Your Hair

Take small sections of hair at the crown of your head and backcomb them using a fine-tooth comb. This will create volume and lift at the roots.

Step 3: Use Hot Rollers or Curling Iron

If you want to achieve loose waves, use hot rollers or a curling iron to curl your hair. For a more defined look, opt for smaller rollers or a smaller barrel curling iron.

Step 4: Tease Your Hair

To add even more volume and texture, gently tease your hair with a comb or brush. Focus on the crown area and the sides of your head.

Step 5: Add Accessories

Now it's time to add some 60s-inspired accessories to complete your look. Choose headbands, scarves, or hair clips that match your outfit and style. Place them strategically in your hair to enhance the retro vibe.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

To ensure your hairstyle stays in place all day, finish off with a generous amount of hairspray. This will help hold the volume and texture you created, giving you a long-lasting 60s look.

Tips for Styling Short Hair 60s

Now that you know the step-by-step process, here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect 60s-inspired hairstyle:

1. Choose the Right Products

Invest in quality volumizing and texturizing products that will help you achieve the desired look. Look for mousses, sprays, and powders specifically designed for short hair.

2. Experiment with Different Accessories

Don't be afraid to try out different accessories to find the ones that suit your style and personality best. Headbands, scarves, and hair clips can instantly transform your look and add that 60s touch.

3. Practice Your Techniques

Styling short hair can be a bit tricky at first, so don't get discouraged if you don't achieve the perfect look right away. Practice your techniques and experiment with different methods until you find what works best for your hair type and length.

4. Get Inspired

Look for inspiration in old magazines, movies, and photographs from the 60s. Take note of the hairstyles you like and try to recreate them using the techniques and tips mentioned in this post.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I achieve a 60s hairstyle with long hair?

A: While short hair was popular in the 60s, you can still achieve a similar look with long hair. Opt for voluminous updos, loose waves, or a sleek and straight hairstyle to achieve a 60s-inspired look.

Q: What if I don't have the right accessories?

A: If you don't have access to 60s-inspired accessories, you can still achieve a retro look by focusing on the hairstyle itself. Use the techniques mentioned in this post to create volume and texture in your hair, and style it in a way that resembles the 60s era.

Q: How long does it take to style short hair 60s?

A: The time it takes to style short hair in a 60s-inspired way depends on your hair length, texture, and the techniques you choose to use. On average, it can take anywhere from 15 minutes to an hour.

Q: Can I use heat styling tools on short hair?

A: Yes, you can use heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners on short hair. Just make sure to use a heat protectant spray before applying any heat to your hair to prevent damage.

Conclusion of How to Style Short Hair 60s

Styling short hair in a 60s-inspired way can be a fun and exciting experience. By following the step-by-step guide and tips mentioned in this post, you can achieve a retro and glamorous look that will make you feel like you've stepped right out of the 60s. So go ahead and embrace your inner vintage diva with a fabulous 60s hairstyle!
