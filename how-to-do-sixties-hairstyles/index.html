How To Do Sixties Hairstyles A Step By Step Guide

Table of Contents

Introduction

The sixties era was known for its iconic fashion and hairstyles. From the bouffants to the beehives, these hairstyles were bold, glamorous, and full of volume. If you're looking to recreate the retro charm of the sixties, this step-by-step guide will help you achieve the perfect sixties hairstyle.

Tools and Products You'll Need

1. Wide-toothed comb

A wide-toothed comb will help you detangle your hair without causing any breakage. It's essential for creating a smooth base for your sixties hairstyle.

2. Bobby pins and hair ties

Bobby pins and hair ties will be your best friends during the hairstyling process. They will help you secure your hair in place and create the desired volume and shape.

3. Teasing brush

A teasing brush will help you achieve the signature sixties volume. It's perfect for backcombing your hair and creating a bouffant or beehive.

4. Hairspray

Hairspray is essential for setting your hairstyle in place and ensuring it lasts all day. Opt for a strong-hold hairspray that will keep your sixties hairstyle intact.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you begin styling, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to work with and ensure your hairstyle lasts longer. If your hair is naturally oily, use a dry shampoo to absorb any excess oil.

Step 2: Create Volume at the Crown

Start by sectioning off the hair at the crown of your head. Gently backcomb this section using a teasing brush to create volume. Once you've achieved the desired height, carefully smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section.

Step 3: Make a Half-Up Ponytail

Next, gather the hair from the sides and back of your head and secure it in a half-up ponytail using a hair tie. This will create a sleek look at the front while leaving the back voluminous.

Step 4: Create a Bouffant or Beehive

If you're going for a bouffant, take small sections of hair from the ponytail and backcomb them. Gently smooth the top layer and pin the sections in place with bobby pins to create a rounded shape. For a beehive, tease the entire ponytail and shape it into a rounded mound, securing it with bobby pins.

Step 5: Style the Remaining Hair

For a classic sixties look, leave the remaining hair loose and voluminous. You can add soft curls or waves using a curling iron or hot rollers. Alternatively, you can create a sleek and straight look by using a flat iron.

Step 6: Set Your Hairstyle with Hairspray

Once you're satisfied with your sixties hairstyle, give it a final spritz of hairspray to set it in place. This will help your hairstyle withstand any weather conditions and stay intact throughout the day.

Conclusion

With these simple steps, you can easily recreate the iconic sixties hairstyles and add a touch of retro glam to your look. Whether you're attending a themed party or simply want to embrace the vintage charm, these hairstyles are sure to turn heads. So go ahead, channel your inner sixties diva, and rock those glamorous locks!
