History and Myth of Short Hair Becoming Fashionable

The history of short hair becoming fashionable can be traced back to ancient civilizations. In ancient Egypt, both men and women wore short hairstyles as a symbol of cleanliness and social status. In ancient Greece, short hair was associated with youth and beauty.

However, during the Victorian era in England, long hair became the norm for women. It was seen as a sign of femininity and modesty. Women would spend hours styling and curling their long locks, often with the help of hairpieces and wigs.

Short hair made a comeback in the 1920s, challenging societal norms and traditional beauty standards. Women who embraced the flapper style were seen as rebellious and progressive. This shift in fashion marked a significant turning point in the history of short hair.

Hidden Secrets of Short Hair Becoming Fashionable

One hidden secret of short hair becoming fashionable is the influence of popular culture. Another hidden secret is the impact of World War I. During the war, many women worked in factories and took on traditionally male roles. The practicality of short hair made it a popular choice among these women.
