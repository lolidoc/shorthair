Frequently Asked Questions about New Short Curly Hairstyles for 2021

1. How do I choose the right short curly hairstyle for my face shape?

When choosing a short curly hairstyle, it's important to consider your face shape. A hairstylist can help you determine which haircut will complement your features the best. Generally, for round faces, longer layers and side-swept bangs can help elongate the face. For square faces, soft curls and layers can soften the angles. For heart-shaped faces, a pixie cut or a bob with side-swept bangs can balance the proportions. And for oval faces, most short curly hairstyles will suit you.

2. How can I prevent frizz in my short curly hair?

To prevent frizz in your short curly hair, it's important to keep it moisturized and well-nourished. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for curly hair. After washing, apply a leave-in conditioner or a curl-enhancing cream to define your curls and reduce frizz. Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can disrupt the curl pattern and cause frizz. Lastly, protect your hair from heat and humidity by using a heat protectant spray and wearing a hat or scarf when necessary.

3. Can I straighten my short curly hair occasionally?

Yes, you can straighten your short curly hair occasionally if you'd like to switch up your look. However, it's important to use heat protectant products and lower heat settings to minimize damage to your curls. Make sure to deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and moisturized. Remember, embracing your natural curls is always a great option, but it's also fun to experiment with different styles from time to time.

4. How often should I trim my short curly hair?

It's recommended to trim your short curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends. Regular trims help to keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage. If you notice any damaged or split ends between trims, it's a good idea to schedule a trim sooner to maintain the health and integrity of your curls.
