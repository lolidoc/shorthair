The Swinging Sixties

The 1960s were a time of cultural revolution, and the hairstyles of the era reflected this spirit of change. One of the most iconic hairstyles of the 60s was the beehive. This voluminous updo, popularized by stars like Audrey Hepburn and Brigitte Bardot, was achieved by backcombing the hair and securing it high on the head with bobby pins.

Another popular hairstyle of the 60s was the pixie cut. This short, boyish haircut was made famous by the likes of Twiggy and Mia Farrow. It was a bold statement that challenged traditional notions of femininity.

The Groovy Seventies

The 1970s saw a shift towards a more relaxed and natural look. One of the most recognizable hairstyles of the decade was the shag. This layered haircut, popularized by stars like Farrah Fawcett and Jane Fonda, featured long, feathered bangs and tousled layers.

Another popular hairstyle of the 70s was the afro. This voluminous, natural hairstyle was embraced by the Black community as a symbol of pride and self-expression. Stars like Pam Grier and Diana Ross helped popularize the afro and make it a fashion statement.

The Influence of Pop Culture

Pop culture played a significant role in shaping the popular hairstyles of the 60s and 70s. Music icons like The Beatles and The Rolling Stones had a major influence on trends. The Beatles' mop-top hairstyles became a global phenomenon, with young people everywhere imitating their look.

In the 70s, disco music and the movie Saturday Night Fever brought about a new wave of hairstyles. The disco era was all about big, glamorous hair. Feathered hair, showcased by stars like John Travolta and Farrah Fawcett, became a symbol of the era.

Tips for Achieving Retro Looks

If you're looking to recreate these iconic hairstyles, there are a few tips to keep in mind. For the beehive, backcombing is key. Use a fine-toothed comb to tease the hair at the crown and then smooth it over to create the desired shape.

To achieve the shag haircut, ask your stylist for long layers and feathered bangs. Styling products like mousse or texturizing spray can help add volume and texture to the hair.

The Enduring Legacy

Even though the 60s and 70s are long gone, their hairstyles continue to inspire and influence current trends. Many celebrities and fashionistas still look to the past for inspiration, whether it's the beehive or the shag.

These hairstyles are a testament to the enduring power of fashion and the way it can shape our culture. So embrace your inner retro diva and rock a vintage hairstyle that pays homage to the iconic looks of the 60s and 70s!
