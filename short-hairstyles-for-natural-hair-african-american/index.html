Short Hairstyles For Natural Hair African American

Written by Vannesa May 06, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to natural hair African American women are known for their versatility and creativity in styling Short hairstyles have become increasingly popular in recent years as they offer ease of maintenance and a bold statement In this article we will explore some of the best short hairstyles for African American women with natural hair in the year 2023

Table of Contents

Introduction

When it comes to natural hair, African American women are known for their versatility and creativity in styling. Short hairstyles have become increasingly popular in recent years, as they offer ease of maintenance and a bold statement. In this article, we will explore some of the best short hairstyles for African American women with natural hair in the year 2023.

1. The Tapered Cut

The tapered cut is a classic short hairstyle that never goes out of style. This haircut is characterized by shorter hair on the sides and back, gradually getting longer on top. It frames the face beautifully and accentuates your natural features. You can rock it with a defined part or go for a more edgy look with an undercut.

2. The Afro Puff

If you're looking for a versatile and effortless hairstyle, the Afro puff is perfect for you. Simply gather your natural hair into a high ponytail at the crown of your head and let your curls shine. You can also accessorize with headbands or hair clips to add a touch of glam.

3. The Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a bold and daring choice that exudes confidence. This short hairstyle is characterized by its short length all around and can be customized to suit your unique facial features. Whether you opt for a sleek and polished look or embrace your natural curls, the pixie cut is sure to turn heads.

4. The Braided Bob

For those who want to add some flair to their short hairstyles, the braided bob is a great option. This hairstyle combines the elegance of a bob with intricate braids for a chic and sophisticated look. You can experiment with different braid patterns and sizes to create a style that is uniquely yours.

5. The Mohawk

The Mohawk is a bold and fierce hairstyle that is popular among African American women. This edgy look features shaved or closely cropped sides with a longer strip of hair in the center. You can style the center strip in various ways, such as braiding, twisting, or even adding hair accessories for added drama.

6. The Finger Coils

If you have natural hair with tight coils or curls, finger coils are a great way to define and enhance your texture. This hairstyle involves twirling small sections of hair around your finger to create neat and springy coils. Not only does it look stylish, but it also helps to minimize frizz and maximize curl definition.

7. The Twist Out

The twist out is a popular hairstyle for African American women with natural hair. It involves dividing damp or dry hair into sections and twisting each section to create defined curls. After a few hours or overnight, you can unravel the twists to reveal beautiful and bouncy curls. This hairstyle can be worn for several days, making it a low-maintenance option.

8. The Wash and Go

If you're looking for a quick and easy hairstyle, the wash and go is a go-to option. Simply wash your hair, apply your favorite leave-in conditioner or styling product, and let your curls air dry. This hairstyle showcases the natural beauty of your hair and requires minimal effort.

9. The Curly Bob

The curly bob is a timeless hairstyle that suits women of all ages. This short hairstyle features a chin-length or slightly longer bob with natural curls or waves. It is a versatile option that can be styled with a middle or side part, and you can even add accessories like hairpins or headbands for a touch of elegance.

10. The Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a traditional African hairstyle that has gained popularity worldwide. This hairstyle involves twisting sections of hair into small knots and securing them close to the scalp. Once the knots are dry, you can unravel them to reveal beautiful and defined curls. Bantu knots can be worn as a standalone hairstyle or as a base for other styles like an updo or half-up half-down look.

Conclusion

Short hairstyles for African American women with natural hair offer a wide range of options to express your personal style and embrace your natural beauty. Whether you prefer a bold and edgy look or a more elegant and sophisticated style, there is a short hairstyle that will suit your taste and lifestyle. Experiment with different cuts, textures, and accessories to find the perfect short hairstyle that makes you feel confident and beautiful.
