How to Cut Your Own Relaxed Hair - A Step-by-Step Guide

When it comes to cutting your own relaxed hair, it's important to have a plan in place before you start. Follow these steps to achieve a successful DIY haircut:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and removing any tangles or knots. Once your hair is clean and detangled, apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to make it easier to comb through.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your wide-tooth comb, divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Secure each section with a hair clip to keep it out of the way while you work.

Step 3: Start with the Back

Begin cutting your hair in the back by releasing one of the sections from the clip. Comb through the hair to ensure it is smooth and tangle-free. Take a small section of hair between your fingers and trim off the desired length. Repeat this process with the remaining sections of hair in the back.

Step 4: Move to the Front

Once you have finished cutting the back of your hair, release one of the front sections from the clip. Comb through the hair and trim off any split ends or damaged hair. Repeat this process with the other front section.

Step 5: Blend and Style

After you have finished cutting all of your hair, release the remaining sections and comb through your hair to ensure it is even and well-blended. Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head for any uneven areas. Style your hair as desired using heat tools or styling products.

Recommendations for Cutting Your Own Relaxed Hair

If you're considering cutting your own relaxed hair, here are some recommendations to keep in mind:

1. Invest in Quality Tools

Having the right tools is essential for achieving a successful DIY haircut. Invest in a pair of sharp hair cutting shears, a wide-tooth comb, and hair clips. Using dull or improper tools can result in uneven cuts and damage to your hair.

2. Start with Small Trims

If you're new to cutting your own hair, start with small trims to get a feel for the process. It's easier to take off more hair later if needed, but it's difficult to fix a haircut that is too short.

3. Take Breaks and Assess Your Progress

Don't be afraid to take breaks during the cutting process to assess your progress. Step away from the mirror and come back with fresh eyes to ensure that your hair is even and well-blended.

4. Embrace Imperfections

Remember that cutting your own hair is a learning experience, and it's okay to make mistakes. Embrace imperfections and use them as an opportunity to improve your skills for future haircuts.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Relaxed Hair

When it comes to cutting your own relaxed hair, here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before cutting your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Wet hair can appear longer and may result in a shorter haircut than desired.

2. Use a Sharp Pair of Shears

Invest in a pair of sharp hair cutting shears to ensure a clean cut. Dull scissors can lead to frayed ends and uneven layers.

3. Take Small Sections

When cutting your hair, take small sections at a time to ensure accuracy and control. This will also prevent you from cutting off too much hair at once.

4. Cut in Small Increments

If you're unsure about the length you want to cut, start with a small increment and gradually trim more if desired. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I cut my own relaxed hair if I have no prior experience?

A: Yes, you can cut your own relaxed hair even if you have no prior experience. However, it's important to start with small trims and practice on a mannequin head or a willing friend before attempting to cut your own hair.

Q: How often should I trim my relaxed hair?

A: It is recommended to trim your relaxed hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy ends and prevent breakage. However, the frequency of trims may vary depending on your hair's individual needs.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut my relaxed hair?

A: It is not recommended to use regular scissors to cut your relaxed hair. Regular scissors are not as sharp as hair cutting shears and can lead to frayed ends and uneven cuts.

Q: How can I fix a mistake if I accidentally cut too much hair?

A: If you accidentally cut too much hair, there are a few options to fix the mistake. You can try styling your hair differently to camouflage the unevenness, or you can visit a professional hairstylist for a corrective cut.
