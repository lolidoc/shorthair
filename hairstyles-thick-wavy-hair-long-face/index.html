Hairstyles for Thick Wavy Hair and a Long Face: Recommendations

If you have thick, wavy hair and a long face shape, here are some hairstyle recommendations that can help you achieve a flattering look:

1. Long Bob with Waves

A long bob, also known as a lob, is a versatile hairstyle that works well for thick, wavy hair. By adding soft waves to your lob, you can create movement and dimension. This hairstyle grazes your shoulders, making it ideal for balancing out a long face shape.

2. Layered Pixie Cut

If you're looking for a shorter hairstyle, a layered pixie cut can be a great option. The layers add texture and volume to your hair, while the shorter length helps reduce the appearance of a long face shape. This edgy and chic style is perfect for those who want a low-maintenance yet stylish look.

3. Wavy Shag

A wavy shag is a classic hairstyle that never goes out of style. With its layers and texture, a shag haircut adds volume and movement to your thick, wavy hair. This hairstyle can help balance out a long face shape and give you a trendy, effortless look.

4. Half-Up Half-Down with Loose Waves

If you want a versatile hairstyle that can be dressed up or down, try a half-up half-down style with loose waves. This hairstyle allows you to show off your natural waves while adding some elegance and sophistication. By leaving some hair down, you can create the illusion of width and balance out a long face shape.

How to Style Hairstyles for Thick Wavy Hair and a Long Face

When it comes to styling hairstyles for thick, wavy hair and a long face shape, here are some steps to follow:

1. Start with Clean, Conditioned Hair

Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your waves hydrated and defined. This will provide a good foundation for styling.

2. Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product, such as a curl-enhancing mousse or a texturizing spray, to damp hair. This will help enhance your natural waves and add volume.

3. Use a Diffuser

Attach a diffuser to your blow dryer and gently dry your hair on a low heat setting. This will help enhance your waves and reduce frizz.

4. Add Finishing Touches

Once your hair is dry, you can add some finishing touches. You can use a curling iron to define any loose waves or add some texture with a sea salt spray.
