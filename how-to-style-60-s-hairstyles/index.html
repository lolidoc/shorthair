The 1960s were a time of great change and rebellion, and one of the most iconic aspects of this era was the hairstyles. From the sleek and sophisticated look of the bouffant to the free-spirited and voluminous styles of the hippie movement, 60's hairstyles were a reflection of the cultural and social shifts happening at the time.

If you're looking to recreate the stylish and unique hairstyles of the 60s, you may be wondering where to start. How do you achieve that perfect beehive or those effortless waves? In this article, we'll explore the history, techniques, and tips for styling 60's hairstyles, so you can rock a retro look with confidence.

History of 60's Hairstyles

The 60s were a time of experimentation and individuality when it came to hairstyles. Women were no longer confined to the traditional norms of the past, and they embraced new and unconventional styles. The beehive, made popular by icons like Audrey Hepburn and Jackie Kennedy, became a symbol of elegance and sophistication. On the other end of the spectrum, the long, flowing locks of the hippie movement represented a rejection of societal norms and a desire for freedom.

Myth: 60's hairstyles were all about high maintenance and intricate styles. While some styles did require a lot of effort and time, there were also simpler and more natural looks that were popular during this era. It's all about finding the style that suits your personal taste and lifestyle.

Hidden Secrets of 60's Hairstyles

One of the hidden secrets of 60's hairstyles is the use of accessories. Headbands, scarves, and hairpins were popular ways to add flair and personality to a hairstyle. Whether you're going for a sleek updo or loose waves, don't be afraid to experiment with different accessories to elevate your look.

Recommendations for Styling 60's Hairstyles

When it comes to styling 60's hairstyles, there are a few recommendations to keep in mind. First, invest in good quality styling tools like a round brush and a curling iron. These tools will help you achieve the voluminous and polished looks that were popular during this era. Second, use products that add texture and hold to your hair, such as mousse or hairspray. These products will help your hairstyle stay in place throughout the day.

Tips for Styling 60's Hairstyles

Now that you have a better understanding of the history and recommendations for styling 60's hairstyles, let's dive into some tips to help you achieve the perfect retro look.

1. Start with clean, dry hair: Before styling your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. This will help the products and tools you use to work more effectively.

2. Use a volumizing product: To achieve the signature volume of 60's hairstyles, use a volumizing mousse or spray on your roots before styling.

3. Tease your hair: Backcombing or teasing your hair at the roots will create the desired height and volume for many 60's hairstyles.

4. Create soft waves: To achieve soft waves, wrap small sections of hair around a curling iron and hold for a few seconds before releasing. Finger-comb the curls for a more natural look.

5. Secure with bobby pins: To keep your hairstyle in place, use bobby pins to secure any loose sections or curls.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I achieve 60's hairstyles on short hair?

A: While some 60's hairstyles are more suited for longer hair, there are still plenty of options for those with shorter hair. Play around with accessories and experiment with different styling techniques to find a look that works for you.

Q: How can I make my 60's hairstyle last all day?

A: To make your 60's hairstyle last all day, use a strong-hold hairspray and avoid touching or brushing your hair too much throughout the day. You can also try pinning up sections of your hair to maintain the shape and volume.

Q: Are there any modern twists on 60's hairstyles?

A: Absolutely! Many modern hairstyles are inspired by the looks of the past. You can add a modern twist to a 60's hairstyle by incorporating braids, twists, or other contemporary elements.

Q: Can I achieve 60's hairstyles without heat styling?

A: Yes, there are heatless styling techniques you can use to achieve 60's hairstyles. For example, you can create soft waves by braiding your hair overnight or using foam rollers.

Conclusion of 60's Hairstyles

Styling 60's hairstyles is a fun and creative way to pay homage to a bygone era. Whether you're looking to recreate the glamour of the beehive or embrace the free-spirited vibes of the hippie movement, there are endless possibilities to explore. With the right tools, techniques, and products, you can achieve a retro look that is uniquely yours.
