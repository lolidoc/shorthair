Tips for Cutting Hair Shorter in Back and Longer in Front

1. Start with clean, dry hair: It is easier to see the natural fall of the hair and make precise cuts when the hair is clean and dry.

2. Use sharp scissors: Dull scissors can cause uneven and jagged cuts. Invest in a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors for the best results.

3. Start with small, gradual cuts: It is easier to remove more hair later if needed, but it is difficult to add hair back once it has been cut. Start with small, gradual cuts and make adjustments as needed.

4. Take your time: Cutting your own hair can be challenging, especially if you are new to the technique. Take your time and work in small sections to ensure accuracy and precision.
