What Was the Dress Style in the 60's?

The Swinging Sixties

The 1960s, often referred to as the "Swinging Sixties," was a decade of cultural and social revolution. It was a time of immense change, and this was reflected in the fashion of the era. The dress style in the 60's was characterized by bold, vibrant colors, playful patterns, and a sense of freedom and individuality.

The Mod Look

One of the most iconic dress styles of the 60's was the mod look. This style was heavily influenced by the British fashion scene and was popularized by fashion icons such as Twiggy and Jean Shrimpton. The mod look featured short, geometrically shaped dresses, often with bold patterns such as polka dots or stripes. These dresses were typically paired with knee-high boots and accessorized with statement jewelry.

Hippie Fashion

Another significant dress style of the 60's was hippie fashion. This style was characterized by loose-fitting, flowing garments made from natural fabrics such as cotton and linen. Maxi dresses and skirts with floral prints were popular choices among the hippie crowd. Fringe details, tie-dye patterns, and bell-bottom pants were also common elements of hippie fashion. Accessories like headbands, oversized sunglasses, and peace sign jewelry completed the look.

The Jackie Kennedy Look

While the mod and hippie styles were popular among the younger generation, the more sophisticated and elegant Jackie Kennedy look was favored by older women. Jacqueline Kennedy, the First Lady of the United States in the early 60's, was known for her timeless style. She often wore tailored suits, pillbox hats, and A-line dresses with matching jackets. The Jackie Kennedy look exuded grace and sophistication.

Key Fashion Trends

In addition to the various dress styles, there were several key fashion trends that defined the 60's. Shift dresses, which were loose and straight-cut, were a popular choice for women of all ages. The mini skirt, famously brought into the mainstream by Mary Quant, became a symbol of the era's liberation and rebellion against traditional norms. The bikini, introduced in the 60's, also gained popularity as women embraced more daring swimwear.

The 60's was also a time of experimentation with different fabrics and textures. Synthetic materials like polyester and PVC were introduced and used to create futuristic and space-age-inspired clothing. Metallic fabrics, bold prints, and bright colors were all characteristic of the fashion of this era.

The Legacy of 60's Fashion

The dress styles of the 60's had a lasting impact on fashion and continue to influence designers and trends today. The mod look, with its bold patterns and playful silhouettes, can still be seen in modern fashion. The bohemian and hippie styles have also made a comeback, with flowy maxi dresses and fringe details being popular choices for festivals and summer fashion.

The 60's was a decade of fashion revolution, where individuality and self-expression were embraced. The dress styles of this era continue to inspire and captivate fashion enthusiasts around the world, making it a truly iconic period in fashion history.
