The 1960s were a decade of revolution and change, and this was reflected in the hairstyles of the time. From the iconic beehive to the popular pixie cut, the hairstyles of the 60s were bold, unique, and full of personality. Whether you were a mod, a hippie, or a rock 'n' roll fan, there was a hairstyle to match your style and make a statement.

During the 60s, there were different pain points when it came to hairstyles. Women were expected to have long, flowing hair, and cutting it short was seen as a radical and rebellious act. Men, on the other hand, were expected to have short, neat haircuts, and growing their hair long was seen as a sign of rebellion and non-conformity.

The hairstyle that defined the 60s was the beehive. This iconic hairstyle, popularized by celebrities like Audrey Hepburn and Brigitte Bardot, featured a high, rounded shape that resembled a beehive. It required a lot of teasing and hairspray to achieve the desired height, and it was often accessorized with a headband or a bow.

In addition to the beehive, there were other popular hairstyles in the 60s. The pixie cut, made famous by actress Mia Farrow, was a short and boyish hairstyle that was both chic and rebellious. The bouffant, characterized by its high volume and backcombing, was another popular choice.

My Personal Experience with 60s Hairstyles

As a fan of vintage fashion, I have always been drawn to the hairstyles of the 60s. I have experimented with the beehive and the pixie cut, and I have found that they are both fun and versatile. The beehive adds a touch of glamour to any outfit, while the pixie cut is edgy and modern.

History and Myth of 60s Hairstyles

The 60s were a time of cultural and social revolution, and this was reflected in the hairstyles of the time. Women were breaking free from traditional gender roles and embracing their individuality, and their hairstyles became a form of self-expression. The beehive, with its bold and dramatic shape, was seen as a symbol of female empowerment and rebellion.

The Hidden Secret of 60s Hairstyles

One of the hidden secrets of 60s hairstyles is the amount of time and effort required to achieve the desired look. The beehive, for example, required hours of teasing, backcombing, and hairspray. It was not a hairstyle that could be achieved quickly or easily, but the end result was worth the effort.

Recommendations for 60s Hairstyles

If you are interested in trying a 60s hairstyle, there are a few recommendations I would make. First, invest in a good teasing comb and hairspray. These two tools are essential for creating the volume and height that are characteristic of 60s hairstyles. Second, practice your technique. Achieving the perfect beehive or bouffant takes time and practice, so don't be discouraged if it doesn't look perfect on your first try. Finally, have fun with it! 60s hairstyles are all about embracing your individuality and making a statement, so don't be afraid to experiment and try new things.

More about 60s Hairstyles

The hairstyles of the 60s were influenced by a variety of factors, including fashion, music, and popular culture. The mod subculture, with its clean lines and geometric shapes, inspired hairstyles that were sleek and streamlined. The hippie movement, with its emphasis on natural beauty and freedom, inspired hairstyles that were long, loose, and flowing.

Tips for 60s Hairstyles

If you want to recreate a 60s hairstyle, there are a few tips that can help you achieve the desired look. First, make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start styling. Second, use a good quality hairspray to help hold your style in place. Third, don't be afraid to experiment with different accessories, such as headbands, bows, and scarves. These can add a touch of authenticity to your look and help you achieve the desired 60s vibe.

Fun Facts about 60s Hairstyles

- The beehive hairstyle was inspired by the shape of a beehive and was first popularized by actress Audrey Hepburn in the movie "Breakfast at Tiffany's".

- The bouffant hairstyle was created by French hairdresser Antoine de Paris and became popular in the 60s thanks to First Lady Jackie Kennedy.

- The Beatles' famous "moptop" hairstyle, with its long, shaggy hair and bangs, became a popular trend in the 60s.

- The pixie cut was popularized by actress Mia Farrow in the movie "Rosemary's Baby" and became a symbol of female empowerment and rebellion.

How to Achieve 60s Hairstyles

If you want to achieve a 60s hairstyle, there are a few steps you can follow. First, start by teasing your hair at the roots to create volume. Next, use a comb or brush to smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section. Finally, use hairspray to hold your style in place and add shine.

What If You Don't Like 60s Hairstyles?

If you don't like 60s hairstyles, there are plenty of other options to choose from. The 60s were a time of experimentation and change, and there were many different hairstyles to suit every taste and style. Whether you prefer short and sleek or long and flowing, there is a hairstyle out there for you.
