Short Afro Styles for Natural Hair

Introduction

Natural hair has always been celebrated for its versatility and unique beauty. Short afro styles have gained popularity in recent years as more people embrace their natural hair texture. In this article, we will explore some trendy and stylish short afro styles that you can try in 2023.

1. Tapered Afro

The tapered afro is a classic and timeless style that never goes out of fashion. It features shorter hair on the sides and back, gradually getting longer towards the top. This style adds definition to your face and showcases your natural curls.

2. Frohawk

The frohawk is a bold and edgy style that combines a mohawk-inspired look with an afro. It involves shaving or cutting the sides short while leaving a strip of longer hair in the middle. This style allows you to experiment with various designs and patterns.

3. Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a traditional African hairstyle that creates beautiful, small, coiled buns all over the head. This style not only looks stunning but also helps to protect your natural hair and promote healthy growth. You can wear the Bantu knots as a temporary style or unravel them for gorgeous curls.

4. Finger Coils

Finger coils are a popular choice for those with shorter natural hair. This style involves twisting small sections of hair around your finger to create defined coils. Finger coils can last for several days and give your hair a structured and polished look.

5. Twist-Out

A twist-out is a versatile style that can be achieved on short afro hair. It involves twisting small sections of hair and allowing them to unravel after they have dried. This creates beautiful, defined curls that can be worn for several days.

6. Afro Puff

The afro puff is a simple yet stylish way to wear your natural hair. This style involves gathering all your hair into a high or low puff at the crown of your head. It is a quick and convenient option for those days when you want to keep your hair off your face.

7. Shaved Sides

If you're looking for a bold and daring look, consider shaving the sides of your afro. This style creates a striking contrast between the longer hair on top and the shaved sides. It's a great way to showcase your individuality and make a statement.

8. Mohawk

The mohawk is a classic hairstyle that can be customized to suit your natural hair. You can either shave or cut the sides short while leaving a strip of longer hair in the middle. The mohawk adds an element of coolness and rebellion to your overall look.

9. Afro-Textured Pixie

If you prefer a shorter and more low-maintenance style, an afro-textured pixie is a great choice. This style involves cutting your hair short all over, showcasing your natural curls and texture. It's a chic and modern option that requires minimal styling.

10. Head Wrap

For those days when you want to switch up your look, consider accessorizing your short afro with a stylish head wrap. Head wraps come in various colors, prints, and fabrics, allowing you to express your personal style while protecting your hair.
