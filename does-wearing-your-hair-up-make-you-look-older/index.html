The History and Myth of Does Wearing Your Hair Up Make You Look Older

The idea that wearing your hair up makes you look older is not a new concept. Throughout history, certain hairstyles have been associated with maturity and elegance. In ancient Greece, for example, women often wore their hair up in elaborate styles such as the chignon or the braided crown, which were seen as symbols of femininity and sophistication.

In more recent times, wearing the hair up has been associated with more formal or professional settings. Updos are often worn at weddings, red carpet events, and in corporate environments. This association may contribute to the belief that wearing your hair up makes you look older, as these occasions typically call for a more polished and mature look.

However, it's important to note that hairstyles and trends are constantly evolving. What may have been considered a mature or formal hairstyle in the past may now be seen as youthful and trendy. The key is to find a hairstyle that makes you feel confident and suits your personal style, regardless of societal norms or perceptions.

The Hidden Secret of Does Wearing Your Hair Up Make You Look Older

While there is no hidden secret that wearing your hair up will automatically make you look older, there is a trick to making an updo look more youthful and fresh. The secret lies in the styling and placement of the updo.

Opt for a looser, more tousled updo rather than a sleek and tight style. This will create a softer and more relaxed look, which can be more youthful. Adding texture and volume to the hair before styling can also help create a more youthful appearance. Consider incorporating braids or twists into the updo, as these can add interest and dimension to the hairstyle.

Another secret is to pay attention to the placement of the updo. Avoid pulling the hair too tightly or placing the updo too high on the head, as this can create a harsh and aging effect. Instead, opt for a lower or side-swept updo, which can be more flattering and youthful.
