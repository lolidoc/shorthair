The Iconic Hairstyles of the 1960s

The 1960s was an era of revolution and self-expression, as reflected in the iconic hairstyles of the time. From the sleek and sophisticated beehive to the voluminous and carefree bouffant, the 1960s hairstyles were all about making a statement. If you're looking to recreate these retro looks, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide.

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you begin styling your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture. Towel-dry your hair gently and apply a heat protectant spray to shield your hair from damage caused by styling tools.

Step 2: Choose Your 1960s Hairstyle

The 1960s offered a variety of hairstyles, so choose the one that speaks to you. If you're aiming for a sophisticated look, go for the classic beehive. For a more casual and playful vibe, opt for the tousled and voluminous bouffant. You can also consider the sleek and straight "Twiggy" haircut or the iconic pixie cut.

Step 3: Create Volume

To achieve the signature volume of 1960s hair, you'll need to tease your hair. Take small sections of hair near the crown of your head and gently backcomb them using a fine-toothed comb. This will create the desired height and fullness.

Step 4: Set Your Hair

Once you've teased your hair to create volume, it's time to set it in place. Use large hot rollers or a curling iron to add loose curls or waves to your hair. Roll the hair away from your face to achieve that classic 1960s look. Leave the rollers in for about 20 minutes to ensure the curls set properly.

Step 5: Brush Out the Curls

After removing the rollers, use a paddle brush or a wide-toothed comb to gently brush out the curls. This will give your hair a soft and tousled appearance, reminiscent of the 1960s hairstyles. Avoid brushing too vigorously to maintain the volume and shape.

Step 6: Add Finishing Touches

To complete your 1960s look, add some finishing touches. Consider using a light hairspray to hold the style in place and prevent frizz. You can also accessorize with a headband, scarf, or hairpin to add an extra touch of retro flair.

Step 7: Embrace the 1960s Attitude

Getting the perfect 1960s hairstyle is not just about the physical aspects; it's also about embracing the attitude of the era. The 1960s were all about confidence, freedom, and breaking boundaries. So, walk tall, rock your retro hair, and let your inner 1960s diva shine.

In Conclusion

Recreating the hairstyles of the 1960s is a fun and stylish way to pay homage to a decade filled with fashion-forward trends. By following these step-by-step instructions and embracing the spirit of the era, you'll be able to achieve the perfect 1960s hairdo. So, put on your favorite vintage dress, turn up the classic tunes, and get ready to transport yourself back in time with your fabulous 1960s hair.
