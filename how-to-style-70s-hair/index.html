The 70s was a decade known for its iconic fashion and hairstyles. One of the most popular hairstyles of that era was the 70s hair. With its voluminous curls, flowing locks, and bohemian vibes, 70s hair has made a comeback in recent years. If you're looking to recreate this retro hairstyle, read on to learn how to style 70s hair.

Many people struggle with styling their hair to achieve that 70s look. They may find it difficult to create the right amount of volume or struggle to get the curls just right. But with a few simple techniques and the right products, you can easily achieve the 70s hair of your dreams.

To style 70s hair, start by washing your hair and applying a volumizing mousse or spray to damp hair. Use a round brush to blow-dry your hair, lifting the roots to create volume. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron or hot rollers to create loose, bouncy curls. Brush out the curls with a wide-tooth comb to create a more natural, lived-in look. Finish off with a spritz of hairspray to hold the style in place.

In conclusion, styling 70s hair is all about creating volume and loose curls. By following the steps mentioned above and using the right products, you can easily achieve the retro look you desire.

How to Style 70s Hair: A Step-by-Step Guide

When it comes to styling 70s hair, there are a few key steps you need to follow. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve that retro look:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and applying a volumizing mousse or spray to damp hair. This will help create the volume needed for the 70s look.

Step 2: Blow-Dry Your Hair

Use a round brush to blow-dry your hair, lifting the roots to create volume. Focus on drying the roots first and then work your way down to the ends.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron or hot rollers to create loose, bouncy curls. Take small sections of hair and wrap them around the curling iron or hot rollers, holding for a few seconds before releasing.

Step 4: Brush Out the Curls

After curling your entire head, use a wide-tooth comb to gently brush out the curls. This will create a more natural, lived-in look.

Step 5: Set the Style

Finish off your 70s hair look with a spritz of hairspray to hold the style in place. Use a light-hold hairspray to avoid weighing down your hair.

How to Style 70s Hair: Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect 70s hair:

Use the Right Products

Invest in a volumizing mousse or spray to create the volume needed for 70s hair. Look for products that are specifically designed to add lift and body to your hair.

Don't Overdo It

While 70s hair is all about volume and curls, be careful not to overdo it. Aim for a natural, lived-in look rather than tight, perfect curls.

Experiment with Accessories

Add some 70s flair to your hairstyle by experimenting with accessories. Try adding a headband, scarf, or flower crown to complete the look.

Embrace Imperfections

70s hair is all about embracing imperfections. Don't worry if your curls aren't perfectly uniform or if you have a few flyaways. Embrace the natural, effortless vibe of the 70s.

Fun Facts About 70s Hair

Did you know that the 70s hair trend was heavily influenced by the hippie movement? The free-spirited, bohemian vibes of the era translated into hairstyles that were loose, voluminous, and full of movement.

How to Style 70s Hair: The Bottom Line

Styling 70s hair may seem daunting at first, but with the right techniques and products, you can easily achieve the retro look you desire. Follow the step-by-step guide and incorporate the tips and tricks mentioned above to create the perfect 70s hairstyle. Embrace the volume, curls, and free-spirited vibe of the era, and have fun experimenting with this iconic hairstyle.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I achieve 70s hair without using heat styling tools?

A: Yes, you can achieve 70s hair without using heat styling tools. Try using foam rollers or braiding your hair overnight to create natural-looking curls.

Q: How can I make my 70s hair last all day?

A: To make your 70s hair last all day, use a strong-hold hairspray and avoid touching or brushing your hair too much throughout the day.

Q: Can I achieve 70s hair on short hair?

A: Yes, you can achieve 70s hair on short hair. Use smaller curling irons or hot rollers to create tighter curls, and focus on adding volume at the roots.

Q: What products should I use to achieve 70s hair?

A: Use a volumizing mousse or spray, a curling iron or hot rollers, and a light-hold hairspray to achieve 70s hair.

Conclusion of How to Style 70s Hair

Styling 70s hair is all about volume, curls, and embracing the free-spirited vibe of the era. Follow the step-by-step guide, incorporate the tips and tricks, and have fun experimenting with this iconic hairstyle. Whether you have long or short hair, you can easily achieve the retro look you desire. So go ahead, channel your inner 70s goddess, and rock that fabulous 70s hair!
