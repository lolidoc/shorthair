What is a Pixie Haircut?

A pixie haircut is a short hairstyle that is typically cut close to the scalp on the sides and back, while leaving some length on the top. It is a versatile haircut that can be customized to suit your individual style and needs. Pixie cuts can be worn sleek and polished or tousled and textured, depending on your preference.

Pixie haircuts have been popular for decades and have been worn by iconic women such as Audrey Hepburn and Mia Farrow. They are a timeless and classic hairstyle that looks great on women of all ages. Whether you have straight, wavy, or curly hair, there is a pixie cut that will suit your hair type and texture.

The History and Myth of Pixie Haircuts

The pixie haircut gets its name from the mythical creature known as a pixie. Pixies are small, mischievous creatures that are often depicted as having short hair. The association between pixies and short hair led to the term "pixie cut" being used to describe the short hairstyle.

In popular culture, pixie haircuts have been associated with rebellion and non-conformity. In the 1960s, the pixie cut became a symbol of female empowerment and was worn by women who wanted to challenge traditional gender roles and societal norms. Today, the pixie cut is still seen as a bold and confident hairstyle that is embraced by women who want to make a statement.
