Question and Answer

Q: Will dyeing my hair at 60 damage it?

A: Dyeing your hair can potentially cause damage, especially if not done properly or if harsh chemicals are used. It's important to consult with a professional stylist who can assess the condition of your hair and recommend the best dyeing technique and products to minimize damage.

Q: Will dyeing my hair at 60 make me look unnatural?

A: Dyeing your hair can be done in a way that looks natural and enhances your overall appearance. A professional stylist can help choose a hair color that complements your skin tone and looks natural on you.

Q: How often will I need to touch up my hair color?

A: The frequency of touch-ups will depend on the type of hair dye used and how quickly your hair grows. It's common to need touch-ups every 4-6 weeks to maintain the desired color.

Q: Can I dye my hair at 60 if I have allergies or sensitive skin?

A: If you have allergies or sensitive skin, it's important to be cautious when dyeing your hair. Consult with a professional stylist who can recommend hair dyes that are safe for sensitive skin or conduct a patch test to check for any reactions.
