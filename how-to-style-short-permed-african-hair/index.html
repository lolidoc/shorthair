How To Style Short Permed African Hair

Short permed African hair can be a challenge to style but with the right techniques and products you can achieve stunning looks that showcase the beauty of your natural curls In this article we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to style your short permed African hair ensuring that it looks fabulous every day

Table of Contents

Introduction

Short permed African hair can be a challenge to style, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve stunning looks that showcase the beauty of your natural curls. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to style your short permed African hair, ensuring that it looks fabulous every day.

Preparation

Before you start styling your short permed African hair, it's essential to ensure that it is in optimal condition. Use a gentle shampoo and moisturizing conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and healthy. Additionally, apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to minimize frizz and promote shine.

1. Define Your Curls

To enhance the definition of your curls, use a curl-enhancing product. Apply it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends. Scrunch your hair gently to encourage the curls to form. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair while maintaining its natural curl pattern.

2. Updo Hairstyles

Short permed African hair can look stunning in updo hairstyles. Try a high bun, a twisted updo, or a stylish half-up, half-down look. Use bobby pins and hair elastics to secure your hair in place, and finish with a lightweight hairspray to keep it in place all day.

3. Braid Out

For a trendy and low-maintenance style, consider a braid out. Start by sectioning your hair into small sections and braiding each section tightly. Leave the braids in overnight or until they are completely dry. Undo the braids gently and use your fingers to separate the curls for a voluminous and textured look.

4. Twist Out

Similar to a braid out, a twist out is another excellent option for styling short permed African hair. Divide your hair into small sections and twist each section tightly. Leave the twists in overnight or until they are fully dry. Untwist the twists carefully, and fluff your hair to create gorgeous defined curls.

5. Finger Coils

Finger coils are a versatile style that works well on short permed African hair. To achieve this look, take small sections of hair and wrap them around your finger, starting from the root and working your way down. This technique creates uniform coils that can be left as they are or slightly separated for a fuller appearance.

6. Headbands and Accessories

Add a pop of color and style to your short permed African hair by incorporating headbands, scarves, or hair accessories. Opt for vibrant colors, bold patterns, or delicate embellishments to make a statement. Headbands can also help keep your hair out of your face while adding a touch of flair.

7. Pixie Cut

If you have a short permed African hair and want a chic and low-maintenance style, consider a pixie cut. This trendy cut allows you to showcase your natural curls while keeping your hair short and stylish. Consult with a professional hairstylist who specializes in textured hair to achieve the perfect pixie cut for your face shape.

8. Pompadour

A pompadour is a classic hairstyle that can be adapted to short permed African hair. Start by styling the sides of your hair closely to your head and gathering the top section into a voluminous twist or roll. Secure it with bobby pins and use a strong-hold hairspray to ensure it stays in place all day.

9. Afro Puff

For a quick and easy style, try an afro puff. Gather your hair into a high ponytail or puff at the crown of your head. Use a hairband or hair tie to secure it in place. Fluff your hair to create volume and shape, and you're ready to go with a stylish and effortless look.

10. Protective Styles

Protective styles such as braids, twists, or wigs can help protect your short permed African hair while allowing it to grow and recover. These styles also provide versatility and allow you to experiment with different looks without damaging your natural curls. Consult with a professional stylist to find the best protective style for your hair type and desired outcome.

Conclusion

Styling short permed African hair can be a fun and creative process. By following these tips and trying out different techniques, you can embrace and enhance the natural beauty of your curls. Remember to keep your hair hydrated, use the right products for your hair type, and consult with professionals for personalized advice. With these styling ideas, you'll be turning heads wherever you go.
