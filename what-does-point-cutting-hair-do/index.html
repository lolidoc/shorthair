History and Myth of Point Cutting Hair

Point cutting hair is not a new technique - it has been used by hairstylists for many years. The technique originated in the 1960s and gained popularity during the punk and grunge movements of the 1970s and 80s. Point cutting was often used to create edgy, textured hairstyles that defied traditional beauty standards.

There is a common myth that point cutting can cause damage to the hair. While it is true that cutting the hair at an angle can remove more hair than a straight cut, when done correctly by a professional, point cutting should not cause any significant damage. In fact, it can actually help to remove split ends and improve the overall health of the hair.

How to Point Cutting Hair

If you're interested in learning how to point cut your own hair, I recommend seeking professional guidance. Point cutting is a technique that requires skill and precision, and it's best to learn from an experienced stylist.

However, if you're feeling adventurous and want to try point cutting at home, here are a few general steps to follow:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into manageable sections.
  3. Hold the scissors at an angle and cut small sections of hair at different lengths.
  4. Work your way through each section, creating layers and texture.
  5. Remember to take your time and be cautious - you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  6. After you've finished point cutting, style your hair as desired.
