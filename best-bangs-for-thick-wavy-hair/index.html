What are Curtain Bangs?

Curtain bangs are a type of bangs that are characterized by their soft, face-framing layers. They are parted down the middle, creating a curtain-like effect. Curtain bangs are versatile and can be worn with any hair length and texture. They are particularly well-suited for those with thick wavy hair, as they blend seamlessly with the natural waves.

Curtain bangs have been popularized by celebrities like Alexa Chung and Brigitte Bardot. They add a touch of femininity and elegance to your look, making them a perfect choice for those with thick wavy hair.

The History and Myth of Curtain Bangs

The history of curtain bangs dates back to the 1960s, when they were popularized by iconic French actress Brigitte Bardot. Bardot's signature hairstyle, which included curtain bangs, became a trendsetter and inspired many women to embrace their natural waves.

Over the years, curtain bangs have remained a popular choice for those with thick wavy hair. They are often associated with a bohemian and effortlessly chic style, making them a favorite among fashion-forward individuals.
