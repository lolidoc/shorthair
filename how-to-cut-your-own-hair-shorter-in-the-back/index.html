Pain Points of Cutting Your Own Hair Shorter in the Back

One of the main pain points of cutting your own hair shorter in the back is the fear of making a mistake and ending up with an uneven or botched haircut. Additionally, it can be challenging to reach the back of your head and achieve a symmetrical cut. However, with some practice and the right method, you can overcome these obstacles and successfully cut your own hair shorter in the back.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Shorter in the Back

To cut your own hair shorter in the back, you will need a few essential tools: a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a handheld mirror, and hair clips to section off your hair. Make sure you have good lighting and a clean, clutter-free space to work in. Follow these steps to achieve a professional-looking haircut:

Step 1: Preparation

Start by washing and drying your hair, ensuring it is free of any tangles or knots. Comb through your hair to remove any snags and make it easier to work with. It is also helpful to wear a shirt or cape that you don't mind getting hair on.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Using hair clips, separate your hair into manageable sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and clipping the top section out of the way. Then, divide the remaining hair into two sections, one on each side of your head. Clip these sections up, leaving the back section exposed.

Step 3: Trim the Back

Hold the handheld mirror in one hand and the hair cutting scissors in the other. Position the mirror so you can see the back of your head. Starting at the nape of your neck, carefully trim small sections of hair at a time, working your way up towards the crown. Be sure to cut straight across and avoid any jagged or uneven lines.

Step 4: Blend the Sides

Once you have trimmed the back to your desired length, unclip the side sections of your hair. Use the mirror to guide you as you trim the sides, blending them with the back. Take small sections at a time and cut vertically, using the previously cut back section as a guide.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Once you have achieved the desired length and shape, remove the hair clips and style your hair as desired. Use a comb or brush to check for any uneven sections and make any necessary adjustments. Remember, it's always better to cut less and gradually trim more if needed.
