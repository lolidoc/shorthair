Exploring the Reasons Behind Hair Thinning at 70

There are several factors that can contribute to hair thinning at 70. One common cause is age-related hormonal changes, particularly a decrease in estrogen and testosterone levels. This hormonal imbalance can lead to hair follicles becoming more sensitive to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone that can shrink hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Another factor that can contribute to hair thinning at 70 is genetics. If you have a family history of hair loss or thinning, you may be more prone to experiencing this issue yourself. Additionally, certain medical conditions, medications, and lifestyle factors such as stress, poor nutrition, and smoking can also contribute to hair thinning.
