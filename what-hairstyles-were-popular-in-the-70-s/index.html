Are you curious about the hairstyles that were popular in the 70's? Well, you're in luck! In this blog post, we will dive deep into the world of 70's hairstyles and explore the trends, history, and myths surrounding them. So, grab your comb and let's get started!

The 70's was a decade known for its bold and experimental fashion and hairstyles. People were looking for ways to express themselves and break away from traditional norms. This led to the rise of unique and unconventional hairstyles that became synonymous with the era.

So, what were the popular hairstyles in the 70's? Let's find out!

In the 70's, one of the most iconic hairstyles was the Afro. This voluminous and natural hairstyle became a symbol of black pride and empowerment. It was embraced by both men and women, and its popularity spread beyond the African-American community. The Afro represented a rebellion against societal beauty standards and a celebration of natural hair.

My Personal Experience with 70's Hairstyles

As a child growing up in the 70's, I vividly remember the influence of disco culture on hairstyles. The classic disco hairstyle was characterized by big, bouncy curls and lots of volume. I would watch my mother spend hours in front of the mirror, teasing her hair and adding copious amounts of hairspray to achieve the perfect disco look. It was a glamorous and exciting time for hairstyles!

The History and Myth of 70's Hairstyles

The 70's was a decade of cultural change and revolution, and hairstyles played a significant role in this transformation. The hippie movement, with its emphasis on peace and freedom, influenced hairstyles like long, flowing locks and braids. These hairstyles represented a rejection of societal norms and a return to nature.

Another popular hairstyle of the 70's was the shag haircut. This layered and textured hairstyle was made famous by celebrities like Jane Fonda and Farrah Fawcett. The shag was a versatile style that could be worn by both men and women and was characterized by its tousled and effortless appearance.

The Hidden Secret of 70's Hairstyles

One hidden secret of 70's hairstyles is the use of hair accessories. Headbands, scarves, and decorative clips were often used to add flair and personality to hairstyles. These accessories were a fun and trendy way to elevate any look and were often seen on fashion icons of the time.

Recommendations for 70's Hairstyles

If you're looking to recreate the iconic hairstyles of the 70's, here are a few recommendations:

1. Embrace your natural hair texture and volume. Don't be afraid to let your hair be wild and free!

2. Experiment with different hair accessories like headbands, scarves, and clips to add a touch of 70's flair to your look.

3. Consider trying out a shag haircut for a trendy and effortless style.

4. If you're feeling bold, go for an Afro hairstyle to make a powerful statement.

Exploring 70's Hairstyles in More Detail

Now let's take a deeper dive into the different hairstyles that were popular in the 70's:

1. The Afro: As mentioned earlier, the Afro was a symbol of black pride and empowerment. It was a natural and voluminous hairstyle that represented a rebellion against societal beauty standards.

2. The Disco Hairstyle: This hairstyle was characterized by big, bouncy curls and lots of volume. It was often seen on the dance floors of disco clubs and represented the glamorous and exciting nature of the disco era.

3. The Shag Haircut: The shag was a layered and textured hairstyle that was popularized by celebrities like Jane Fonda and Farrah Fawcett. It was a versatile style that could be worn by both men and women and had a tousled and effortless appearance.

4. Long, Flowing Locks: Inspired by the hippie movement, long, flowing locks were a popular choice in the 70's. This hairstyle represented a rejection of societal norms and a return to nature.

Fun Facts about 70's Hairstyles

1. The popularity of the Afro hairstyle led to a surge in the sale of Afro picks, a comb specifically designed for styling and maintaining the voluminous hair.

2. The iconic Farrah Fawcett hairstyle, with its feathered and layered look, became one of the most requested hairstyles in salons during the 70's.

3. The Beehive hairstyle, which originated in the 60's but still had some popularity in the 70's, was characterized by its tall and voluminous shape, resembling a beehive.

4. The punk movement of the late 70's introduced edgier and more rebellious hairstyles, such as brightly colored hair and shaved heads.

How to Achieve 70's Hairstyles

If you're looking to recreate the iconic hairstyles of the 70's, here are a few tips:

1. Embrace your natural hair texture and use products that enhance volume and texture.

2. Experiment with different styling techniques like teasing, curling, and braiding to achieve the desired look.

3. Use hair accessories like headbands, scarves, and clips to add a touch of 70's flair to your hairstyle.

4. Consider getting a haircut that is reminiscent of the 70's, such as a shag or layered hairstyle.

What If You Want to Try 70's Hairstyles Today?

If you're feeling inspired by the hairstyles of the 70's and want to try them out today, go for it! Fashion is all about self-expression and having fun with your personal style. You can take inspiration from the 70's and put your own modern twist on it. Remember, confidence is key!

Question and Answer

Q: Were 70's hairstyles only popular in the United States?

A: No, 70's hairstyles had a global influence and were popular in many countries around the world.

Q: Can I achieve 70's hairstyles with short hair?

A: Yes, there are adaptations of 70's hairstyles that can be done with short hair. Consult with a hairstylist for the best options for your hair length.

Q: Are there any celebrities known for their 70's hairstyles?

A: Yes, celebrities like Farrah Fawcett, Jane Fonda, and Diana Ross were known for their iconic 70's hairstyles.

Q: Can men wear 70's hairstyles?

A: Absolutely! Many 70's hairstyles, such as the Afro and shag haircut, were popular among men as well.

Conclusion of 70's Hairstyles

In conclusion, the 70's was a decade of bold and experimental hairstyles. From the Afro to the disco hairstyle, there were numerous iconic looks that defined the era. Whether you're looking to recreate these hairstyles or simply draw inspiration from them, the 70's hairstyles continue to inspire and influence the world of fashion and hair today.
