The 70s Hair Flip Trend

The 70s hair flip is a classic hairstyle that has made a comeback in recent years. It is characterized by voluminous, bouncy hair that is flipped out at the ends. This retro style adds a touch of glamour and playfulness to any look. Whether you're going for a casual or formal event, mastering the 70s hair flip can instantly elevate your style.

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start styling, it's important to prep your hair properly. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that add volume and texture. Towel dry your hair gently, avoiding any rough movements that can cause frizz. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to the roots for added lift.

Blow-Drying Techniques

The key to achieving the perfect 70s hair flip lies in the blow-drying technique. Start by flipping your wet hair upside down and gently drying it with a diffuser attachment. This will help create volume and enhance the natural texture of your hair. Once your hair is about 80% dry, flip it back up and continue blow-drying with a round brush. Focus on the ends of your hair, rolling them outwards to create the signature flip.

Using Hot Tools

If you have straight or fine hair, you may need some help from hot tools to achieve the desired 70s hair flip. Use a large barrel curling iron or a straightening iron with rounded edges to curl the ends of your hair outwards. Start from the mid-lengths and work your way down to the ends. Hold the iron in place for a few seconds before releasing the curl. Repeat this step all around your head to create a uniform flip.

Teasing for Volume

To add extra volume and hold to your hair, teasing is essential. Take small sections of hair near the crown and backcomb them gently towards the roots. This will create a cushion of volume, making your hair look fuller and bouncier. Remember to be gentle while teasing and use a comb specifically designed for teasing to avoid damaging your hair.

Finishing Touches

Once you've achieved the perfect 70s hair flip, it's time to set it in place. Use a light hold hairspray to lock in the volume and keep your hair in shape throughout the day. Avoid using too much product as it can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy. If you want a more polished look, you can smooth out the top layers of your hair with a boar bristle brush for a glossy finish.

Styling Options

The 70s hair flip can be styled in various ways to suit different occasions. For a casual look, leave your hair down and tousle it gently with your fingers for a relaxed, boho vibe. If you're attending a formal event, consider adding a deep side part and pinning one side of the hair behind your ear for a more polished look. You can also accessorize with headbands, scarves, or hair clips to add a touch of flair to your hairstyle.

Conclusion

The 70s hair flip is a timeless hairstyle that exudes charm and elegance. With the right techniques and products, you can easily recreate this retro look and make it your own. Whether you're going for a casual or formal vibe, the 70s hair flip is a versatile style that can elevate any outfit. So, go ahead and embrace your inner disco diva with this iconic hairstyle!
