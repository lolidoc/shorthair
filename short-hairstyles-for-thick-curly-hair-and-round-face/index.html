Short Bob

A short bob is a classic and versatile hairstyle that works well for thick curly hair and round faces. This hairstyle is perfect for adding volume and movement to your hair, while also elongating the appearance of your face. To create a short bob, ask your hairstylist to cut your hair to chin-length or slightly below, and add layers to create texture and movement. You can also opt for a side part or bangs to further enhance the shape of your face.

Pixie Cut

A pixie cut is a bold and chic hairstyle that can work wonders for thick curly hair and round faces. This short hairstyle is perfect for adding volume and definition to your hair, while also framing your face and highlighting your features. To create a pixie cut, ask your hairstylist to cut your hair short on the back and sides, and leave some length on top for added texture and versatility. You can also experiment with different styling products and techniques to create a variety of looks.

Curly Shag

A curly shag is a trendy and stylish hairstyle that is perfect for thick curly hair and round faces. This hairstyle is all about adding texture, volume, and movement to your hair, while also flattering the shape of your face. To create a curly shag, ask your hairstylist to cut your hair in layers, with shorter layers on top and longer layers at the bottom. This will help to create a tousled and effortless look that is perfect for both day and night.

Asymmetrical Bob

An asymmetrical bob is a modern and edgy hairstyle that can work wonders for thick curly hair and round faces. This hairstyle is perfect for adding dimension and visual interest to your hair, while also elongating the appearance of your face. To create an asymmetrical bob, ask your hairstylist to cut your hair shorter on one side and longer on the other, and add layers to create texture and movement. You can also experiment with different styling techniques to create a variety of looks.

The Best Short Hairstyles for Thick Curly Hair and Round Faces

When it comes to finding the best short hairstyles for thick curly hair and round faces, it's important to consider your individual style, preferences, and hair texture. Here are some tips to help you find the perfect hairstyle:

2. Consider your lifestyle and how much time you are willing to spend on styling your hair. Some short hairstyles require more maintenance and styling than others.

3. Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for your hair. This can help to enhance your natural curls and add definition.

4. Don't be afraid to try something new and take risks with your hairstyle. Sometimes, the best way to find the perfect hairstyle is to step outside of your comfort zone and try something different.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I straighten my thick curly hair before trying out these short hairstyles?

A: Yes, you can straighten your hair before trying out these short hairstyles. However, keep in mind that your natural hair texture will affect the final look of the hairstyle. It's always best to work with your natural hair texture and embrace your curls.

Q: Can I add highlights or color to my thick curly hair?

A: Yes, you can add highlights or color to your thick curly hair to enhance the look of your short hairstyle. However, it's important to consult with a professional hairstylist who specializes in curly hair to ensure that the color is applied correctly and won't damage your hair.

Q: How often should I trim my thick curly hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your thick curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your short hairstyle. Regular trims will help to prevent split ends and breakage, and keep your hair looking its best.

Q: Can I use heat styling tools on my thick curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use heat styling tools on your thick curly hair, but it's important to use them sparingly and with caution. Excessive heat can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Always use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools, and avoid using high heat settings.
