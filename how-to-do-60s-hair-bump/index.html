How To Create A 60S Hair Bump A Step By Step Guide

Introduction

Are you a fan of vintage hairstyles? Do you want to add a touch of retro glamour to your look? Look no further! In this tutorial, we will guide you through the process of creating a fabulous 60s hair bump. This iconic hairstyle was popularized by stars like Brigitte Bardot and Jackie Kennedy, and it continues to be a timeless and elegant choice for any occasion. So, grab your teasing comb and let's get started!

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before creating the bump, it's important to prepare your hair. Start by washing and conditioning it with products suitable for your hair type. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to add texture and body. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create volume at the roots. This will provide a solid foundation for the bump.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: the crown, the sides, and the back. Clip the side and back sections out of the way for now. The crown section will be the main focus of our styling process.

Step 3: Tease Your Hair

Take a small section of hair at the crown and hold it straight up. Using a teasing comb, gently backcomb the hair towards the roots. This will create volume and texture. Repeat this process for the rest of the crown section, gradually working your way towards the front.

Step 4: Smooth the Teased Hair

After teasing, gently smooth the top layer of hair to hide the teased sections. Use a soft-bristle brush or a comb to do this. Be careful not to brush too vigorously as it may undo the teasing. The goal is to create a smooth surface while maintaining the volume underneath.

Step 5: Secure the Bump

Once you're satisfied with the height and shape of the bump, secure it in place. Take a section of hair from each side of the bump and gently twist them towards the back. Use bobby pins to secure the twists at the back of your head. This will hold the bump in place throughout the day.

Step 6: Style the Remaining Hair

Now that the bump is secured, you can style the rest of your hair. You can leave it down, create loose waves, or opt for an elegant updo. The choice is yours! Just make sure to blend the styled hair with the bump for a cohesive look.

Step 7: Finishing Touches

To give your 60s hair bump that extra touch of retro glamour, consider adding a decorative hair accessory. A thin headband, a sparkly barrette, or a silk scarf can all enhance the overall look. Experiment with different options to find the one that suits your style best.

Step 8: Set Your Style

To ensure that your hair bump lasts all day, use a hairspray to set the style. Hold the can at arm's length and mist it over your hair, paying special attention to the bump. This will help maintain the volume and keep your hair in place throughout the day.

Conclusion

Creating a 60s hair bump is a fun and stylish way to add a vintage touch to your look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a glamorous hairstyle that will turn heads wherever you go. So, embrace your inner retro diva and give this iconic hairstyle a try!
