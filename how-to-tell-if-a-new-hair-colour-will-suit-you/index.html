How To Tell If A New Hair Colour Will Suit You

Introduction

Changing your hair colour can be an exciting way to transform your look and boost your confidence. However, finding the right hair colour that suits you can be a daunting task. With so many options available, it's important to consider several factors before taking the plunge. In this article, we will explore some tips on how to tell if a new hair colour will suit you.

1. Determine Your Skin Tone

One of the most important factors to consider when choosing a new hair colour is your skin tone. Skin tones can be categorized as warm, cool, or neutral. Warm skin tones generally have yellow, peach, or golden undertones, while cool skin tones have pink, red, or blue undertones. Neutral skin tones have a balance of both warm and cool undertones. Knowing your skin tone will help you choose a hair colour that complements your complexion.

2. Consider Your Eye Color

Another factor to consider is your eye color. Certain hair colours can make your eyes pop and enhance their natural beauty. For example, if you have blue eyes, warm hair colours like golden blonde or copper can be flattering. On the other hand, if you have brown eyes, you can experiment with a wide range of hair colours, including warm browns, rich reds, or even vibrant purples.

3. Assess Your Natural Hair Color

Your natural hair color can also play a role in determining which hair colour will suit you best. If you have a fair complexion, going for a dramatic dark hair color may wash you out. Instead, opt for shades that are a few shades lighter or darker than your natural color. If you have darker skin, you can pull off a wider range of hair colors, including both dark and light shades.

4. Consider Your Lifestyle

When choosing a new hair color, it's important to consider your lifestyle and how much time and effort you are willing to invest in maintenance. Some hair colors require more frequent touch-ups and special care to maintain their vibrancy. If you have a busy schedule or prefer low-maintenance hair, it's best to choose a hair color that will require minimal upkeep.

5. Seek Professional Advice

If you're unsure about which hair color will suit you best, it's always a good idea to seek professional advice. A professional hairstylist can assess your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair color to recommend the most flattering shades for you. They can also provide insights on the latest trends and techniques that may suit your desired look.

6. Use Virtual Hair Color Apps

If visiting a salon is not an option, you can make use of virtual hair color apps to get a glimpse of how different hair colors would look on you. These apps use augmented reality technology to virtually apply different hair colors to your photo, allowing you to see the results before making a commitment.

7. Consider a Temporary Option

If you're still unsure about committing to a permanent hair color change, you can consider trying temporary options first. Temporary hair dyes or color sprays can allow you to experiment with different shades without the long-term commitment. This way, you can test different colors and see which ones suit you best.

8. Take Inspiration from Celebrities

Celebrities often experiment with different hair colors, making them a great source of inspiration. Look for celebrities with similar skin tones and eye colors as you and see how different hair colors complement their features. This can give you a better idea of what may suit you.

9. Consider Your Personality and Style

Your personality and personal style should also play a role in choosing a new hair color. If you're someone who loves bold and vibrant looks, you may opt for vibrant hues like purple or blue. If you prefer a more subtle change, natural-looking shades like honey blonde or chocolate brown may be more suitable.

10. Embrace the Change

Lastly, don't be afraid to embrace change and step out of your comfort zone. Trying a new hair color can be a fun and exciting experience, and even if it doesn't turn out exactly as you envisioned, remember that hair color can always be adjusted or changed with time.

Conclusion

Choosing a new hair color that suits you can be a challenging task, but by considering factors such as your skin tone, eye color, natural hair color, lifestyle, and seeking professional advice, you can find a color that enhances your natural beauty and boosts your confidence. Remember to embrace the change and have fun experimenting with different shades!
