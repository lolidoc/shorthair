How to Make a 60's Beehive Hairstyle

Introduction

The 60's beehive hairstyle is an iconic and glamorous look that gained popularity in the 1960s. This voluminous updo, resembling a beehive, was sported by celebrities like Audrey Hepburn and Brigitte Bardot. Recreating this classic hairstyle is a fun way to embrace the retro vibes and add a touch of vintage charm to your look. In this tutorial, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of achieving the perfect 60's beehive hairstyle.

What You'll Need

Before you start, gather the following tools and products:

  • Teasing comb
  • Bobby pins
  • Hairspray
  • Hairbrush
  • Hair elastic
  • Hair donut or sock bun

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by brushing your hair to remove any knots or tangles. Apply a small amount of volumizing mousse or spray to add texture and grip to your hair. This will make it easier to tease and style later on.

Step 2: Tease the Crown

Take a section of hair from the crown of your head and hold it straight up. Using a teasing comb, gently backcomb the hair towards the scalp in short strokes. Repeat this process with small sections of hair until you achieve the desired height and volume.

Step 3: Create a Ponytail

Gather all of your hair, including the teased section, into a high ponytail at the crown of your head. Secure it with a hair elastic.

Step 4: Insert the Hair Donut

If you're using a hair donut, slide it onto the ponytail, positioning it near the base. Ensure that the donut is completely covered by your hair.

Step 5: Spread and Pin the Hair

Spread the ponytail evenly around the hair donut, covering it completely. Secure the ends of your hair with bobby pins, tucking them under the donut to create a neat and polished look.

Step 6: Smooth the Sides

Smooth down the sides of your hair with a brush or your fingers, ensuring that there are no visible bumps or flyaways. Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands.

Step 7: Finishing Touches

Spray your entire hairstyle with a generous amount of hairspray to ensure it holds throughout the day or evening. For extra hold and shine, you can also apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to the ends of your hair.

Step 8: Accessories (Optional)

To complete the 60's beehive look, you can add some accessories like a headband, a silk scarf, or a decorative hairpin. These accessories will enhance the vintage charm and make your hairstyle stand out even more.

Step 9: Final Look

Take a moment to admire your stunning 60's beehive hairstyle in the mirror. You've successfully recreated a classic look that exudes elegance and sophistication.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips to help you perfect your beehive hairstyle:

  • Experiment with different teasing techniques to achieve the desired volume.
  • Use hairspray throughout the styling process to help keep your hair in place.
  • Don't be afraid to play around with the shape and size of your beehive to suit your personal style.
  • If you have thin or fine hair, consider using hair extensions or a volumizing powder to add extra fullness.

Conclusion

The 60's beehive hairstyle is a timeless and glamorous look that never goes out of style. By following these step-by-step instructions and using the right tools and products, you can easily recreate this retro hairstyle. Whether you're attending a themed party or simply want to add a touch of vintage charm to your everyday look, the 60's beehive is a perfect choice. Embrace the elegance and sophistication of the past with this iconic hairstyle.
