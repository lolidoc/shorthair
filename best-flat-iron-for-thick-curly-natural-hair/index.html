What is the Best Flat Iron for Thick Curly Natural Hair?

The best flat iron for thick curly natural hair is a tool that can effectively straighten this hair type without causing damage. It should have adjustable heat settings, tourmaline or ceramic plates, and be wide enough to handle thick hair in one pass.

The History and Myth of Best Flat Iron for Thick Curly Natural Hair

The history of flat irons dates back to ancient times, with evidence of their use in ancient Egypt and Greece. These early flat irons were made of metal and heated over an open flame. They were used to straighten and style hair, much like modern flat irons.

Over the years, flat irons have evolved and become more advanced. Today, there are countless options available on the market, each claiming to be the best for thick curly natural hair. However, there are also many myths surrounding flat irons and their effects on this hair type.

One common myth is that using a flat iron on thick curly natural hair will permanently damage the hair. While excessive heat can cause damage, using a flat iron with adjustable heat settings and proper technique can minimize the risk. It's important to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using the flat iron on wet or damp hair.

Another myth is that flat irons can completely straighten thick curly natural hair and make it look like straight hair. While a flat iron can temporarily straighten the hair, it cannot change the natural texture or curl pattern. Best Flat Iron for Thick Curly Natural Hair: Tips for Success

When using a flat iron on thick curly natural hair, there are a few tips that can help to achieve the best results:

1. Start with clean, dry hair: Flat ironing damp or wet hair can cause damage and reduce the effectiveness of the straightening process. Make sure your hair is completely dry before using a flat iron.

2. Use a heat protectant spray: Applying a heat protectant spray before using a flat iron can help to protect the hair from heat damage. This spray forms a barrier between the hair and the heat, reducing the risk of damage.

3. Section your hair: To ensure even heat distribution and thorough straightening, it's important to section your hair before using a flat iron. This allows you to work on small sections at a time, ensuring each strand is straightened.

4. Use the right temperature: Different hair types require different temperatures to achieve optimal results. Thick curly natural hair can be resistant to heat, so it's important to find a flat iron that can reach high temperatures if needed.
