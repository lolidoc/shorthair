How To See If A Hair Colour Will Suit Me

Table of Contents

Introduction

Choosing a new hair colour can be an exciting and transformative experience. However, it's important to consider whether the chosen colour will suit you before taking the plunge. Luckily, there are several ways to determine whether a hair colour will complement your skin tone, eye colour, and overall appearance. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks to help you make an informed decision about your next hair colour.

Understanding Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone plays a crucial role in determining which hair colours will suit you best. Generally, skin tones fall into three categories: warm, cool, and neutral. Warm-toned individuals have golden or peachy undertones, while cool-toned individuals have pink or blue undertones. Neutral-toned individuals have a combination of both. To determine your skin tone, look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear green, you likely have warm undertones, while blue veins indicate cool undertones.

Choosing the Right Shade

Once you've determined your skin tone, it's time to choose a hair colour that will complement it. If you have warm undertones, opt for shades with golden or reddish hues, such as caramel or copper. Cool undertones pair well with ash or platinum blondes, as well as cool browns like espresso or ash brown. If you have a neutral skin tone, you have the flexibility to experiment with both warm and cool shades.

Consider Your Eye Colour

Another important factor to consider when choosing a hair colour is your eye colour. Certain shades can make your eyes pop and enhance your overall appearance. For example, if you have blue eyes, warm hair colours like golden blondes or coppery reds can create a beautiful contrast. Green or hazel eyes often pair well with warm browns or auburn shades. Brown eyes can pull off a wide range of colours, including both warm and cool tones.

Try Virtual Hair Colour Tools

If you're still unsure about which hair colour will suit you, consider using virtual hair colour tools available online. These tools allow you to upload a photo of yourself and virtually try on different hair colours. While they may not be 100% accurate, they can give you a general idea of how a specific shade will look on you. Experiment with different colours and styles to find the perfect match.

Consult with a Professional

If you're hesitant about making a decision on your own, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional hairstylist. They have the knowledge and experience to guide you in choosing a hair colour that will enhance your features and suit your complexion. They can also provide valuable advice on maintenance and aftercare to ensure your new hair colour stays vibrant and healthy.

Consider Temporary Options

If you're still unsure about committing to a permanent hair colour change, consider trying temporary options. Temporary hair dyes, such as wash-out colours or hair chalk, allow you to experiment with different shades without the long-term commitment. This way, you can see how a particular colour looks on you before fully committing to it.

Take Your Personality into Account

Your personality and personal style should also be taken into account when choosing a hair colour. If you're someone who likes to stand out and make a bold statement, vibrant and unconventional colours like pink or purple might be the perfect choice for you. On the other hand, if you prefer a more natural and subtle look, opt for shades that are closer to your natural hair colour.

Consider Your Lifestyle

Before choosing a hair colour, consider your lifestyle and the level of maintenance you're willing to commit to. Some colours may require more frequent touch-ups and special care to maintain their vibrancy. If you have a busy schedule or prefer low-maintenance hair, opt for colours that are closer to your natural shade or choose low-maintenance techniques like balayage or highlights.

Seek Inspiration

If you're still unsure about which hair colour to choose, seek inspiration from magazines, social media, or even celebrities. Look for individuals with similar skin tones and eye colours and see how different hair colours suit them. Save images of the looks you love and bring them to your hairstylist for reference. They can help tailor the colour to suit your unique features.

Confidence is Key

Ultimately, the most important factor in determining whether a hair colour will suit you is your confidence. If you feel good about yourself and your chosen colour, it will undoubtedly enhance your overall appearance. Trust your instincts and choose a hair colour that makes you feel beautiful and confident in your own skin.

By considering your skin tone, eye colour, personality, and lifestyle, as well as seeking guidance from professionals and using virtual tools, you can make an informed decision about which hair colour will suit you best. Embrace the opportunity to transform your look and express your unique style with a hair colour that enhances your natural beauty.
