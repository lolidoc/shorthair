How to Wear Your Hair Up at 60 - A Personal Experience

When I turned 60, I started to feel self-conscious about my hair. It was thinning and losing its luster, and I didn't know how to style it in a way that made me feel confident. But after doing some research and experimenting with different updo styles, I finally found a look that I love.

The first step in achieving the perfect updo is to start with clean, dry hair. I like to use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to my thinning hair. Once my hair is dry, I apply a small amount of styling product to add texture and hold. Then, using a comb, I gently tease the roots of my hair to create volume.

Next, I gather my hair into a low ponytail and twist it into a bun. I secure the bun with bobby pins, making sure to hide the pins under the hair. To finish off the look, I spritz my hair with a light-hold hairspray to keep everything in place.

Overall, wearing my hair up at 60 has been a game-changer for me. Not only does it make me feel more put-together and elegant, but it also gives me a confidence boost. I love how versatile updos can be - they can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion.
