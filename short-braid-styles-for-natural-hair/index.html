Short Braid Styles for Natural Hair

Introduction

Natural hair is versatile and beautiful, and short braids can be a great way to embrace and showcase your natural texture. Whether you have recently big chopped or have always rocked short hair, there are numerous braid styles that you can try to switch up your look. In this article, we will explore some trendy and easy-to-maintain short braid styles for natural hair in 2023.

1. Box Braids

Box braids are a classic and timeless protective hairstyle that works well for short hair. They involve sectioning your hair into small boxes and braiding each section from the root to the ends. Box braids can be worn in various lengths, including short bob-length styles, which look chic and stylish.

2. Cornrows

Cornrows are another popular braid style for short natural hair. They involve braiding your hair close to the scalp in straight rows. Cornrows can be done in various patterns and designs, allowing you to get creative with your hairstyle. They are low-maintenance and can last for several weeks with proper care.

3. Twists

Twists, also known as two-strand twists, are a great way to add texture and dimension to short natural hair. They involve twisting two sections of hair together to create a rope-like effect. Twists can be worn as is or unraveled to create a twist-out hairstyle, giving you versatility and multiple styling options.

4. Micro Braids

Micro braids are tiny, delicate braids that can be a stunning option for short natural hair. They require a skilled braider to create small and neat braids throughout your hair. Micro braids can be left loose or styled into updos, ponytails, or even buns for a sophisticated look.

5. Halo Braid

The halo braid is a beautiful and elegant braid style that encircles the head like a crown. It works well for short natural hair, giving the illusion of a longer and fuller hairstyle. The halo braid can be achieved by braiding small sections of hair around the head and securing them with bobby pins or hair ties.

6. Jumbo Braids

Jumbo braids are large-sized braids that are perfect for making a bold statement with your short natural hair. They are created by sectioning the hair into bigger parts and braiding them from the root to the ends. Jumbo braids are versatile and can be worn in various styles, such as ponytails or buns.

7. Feed-In Braids

Feed-in braids, also known as invisible braids, are a popular braid style that can give the illusion of natural hair growth. They involve adding extensions to your natural hair gradually as you braid, creating a seamless and natural look. Feed-in braids can be done in various patterns and lengths, allowing you to customize the style to your preference.

8. Ghana Braids

Ghana braids, also known as Ghana cornrows or banana braids, are a traditional African braid style that can be adapted for short natural hair. They involve braiding the hair close to the scalp in intricate patterns, creating a beautiful and eye-catching hairstyle. Ghana braids can be worn for several weeks and are a great option for protective styling.

9. Faux Locs

Faux locs are a trendy and stylish braid style that can be achieved on short natural hair. They involve wrapping sections of hair with extensions to create the appearance of dreadlocks. Faux locs can be worn in various lengths and thicknesses, allowing you to experiment with different looks without the commitment of permanent locs.

10. Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a versatile and easy-to-create braid style for short natural hair. They involve twisting small sections of hair and wrapping them into small knots close to the scalp. Bantu knots can be worn as is or unraveled to create beautiful and defined curls. They are a great option for achieving a heatless curl pattern.
