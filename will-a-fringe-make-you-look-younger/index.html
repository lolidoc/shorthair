What is a Fringe?

A fringe, also known as bangs, is a section of hair that is cut and styled to hang over the forehead. Fringes can be worn in a variety of lengths and styles, from short and blunt to long and wispy. They can be straight across, side-swept, or parted in the middle.

Historically, fringes have been a popular hairstyle choice for both men and women. In ancient Egypt, both men and women would wear fringes to protect their foreheads from the harsh sun. Fringes also became popular in the 1920s as part of the flapper style, and they have remained a staple in the fashion world ever since.

Another hidden secret of fringes is that they can help to soften your facial features and make you look more feminine. If you have a strong jawline or prominent forehead, a fringe can help to soften these features and create a more delicate and feminine appearance.
