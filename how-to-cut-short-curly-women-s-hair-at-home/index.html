How to Cut Short Curly Women's Hair at Home: Understanding the Basics

When it comes to cutting short curly women's hair at home, it is important to understand the basics of curly hair and how it differs from other hair types. Curly hair tends to shrink when dry, so it is essential to cut it when it is in its natural state to avoid any surprises once it dries.

One of the key techniques for cutting curly hair is to work in small sections. This allows you to see the natural shape and texture of your curls and ensures an even haircut. Start by dividing your hair into four sections: front, back, and two sides. Then, working with one section at a time, use sharp scissors to trim the ends, following the shape of your curls.

Another important aspect of cutting curly hair is to avoid cutting too much at once. Curly hair tends to bounce up when it dries, so it is better to err on the side of caution and trim less rather than more. You can always go back and make additional adjustments if needed.
