Listicle of Best Mid Length Hair for Over 50

1. The Bob: A classic and timeless option, the bob is a versatile mid length hair style that can be tailored to suit your individual needs and preferences. Whether you prefer a sleek and polished look or something more tousled and relaxed, the bob can be easily customized to flatter your face shape and hair type.

2. The Layered Cut: Adding layers to your mid length hair can help to create movement and texture, making it appear fuller and more voluminous. This can be especially beneficial for women with thin or fine hair, as it can help to add body and bounce.

3. The Shag: The shag is a trendy and modern mid length hair style that has been popularized by celebrities such as Jane Fonda and Goldie Hawn. It features choppy layers and fringe, creating a relaxed and effortless look.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I still wear my hair long if I'm over 50?

A: Absolutely! There's no rule that says you have to cut your hair short as you age. However, if you find that long hair is difficult to manage or doesn't suit your changing features, you may want to consider a mid length hair style.

Q: Will a mid length hair style make me look older?

A: Not at all! In fact, a well-chosen mid length hair style can help to enhance your natural beauty and make you look more youthful. It's all about finding the right cut and style that suits your individual needs and preferences.

Q: Can I still color my hair if I'm over 50?

A: Of course! Many women over 50 choose to color their hair to cover gray or add dimension and depth. Whether you want to embrace your natural gray or experiment with highlights or lowlights, there are plenty of options to choose from.

Q: How often should I get my mid length hair cut?

A: It depends on your individual hair type and how quickly it grows. On average, most women find that a mid length hair style needs to be trimmed every six to eight weeks to maintain its shape and style.
