How to Cut Permed Hair from Natural Hair: A Step-by-Step Guide

When it comes to cutting permed hair from natural hair, it's important to have a clear understanding of the process and the techniques involved. In this section, we'll dive deeper into the step-by-step guide on how to cut permed hair from natural hair.

Step 1: Gather the Necessary Tools

Before you start cutting your permed hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools at hand. This includes sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. Having the right tools will make the process easier and ensure that you achieve the desired results.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to section your hair. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then create two sections on either side. Secure each section with hair clips to keep them out of the way as you work.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Now that your hair is sectioned, it's time to start cutting. Begin by taking a small section of hair and combing it out to remove any tangles. Hold the hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut off the permed ends. Remember to cut small amounts at a time and work your way up. This will help you achieve a more even result and prevent any drastic changes.

Step 4: Make Adjustments

After you've finished cutting one section, release the clipped hair and comb through it to see if any adjustments are needed. If you notice any unevenness or areas that require further trimming, go ahead and make the necessary adjustments. Remember to take your time and be patient throughout the process.

What is Permed Hair?

Permed hair, also known as a permanent wave, is a chemical process that adds texture and curl to straight hair. It involves the use of chemicals to break and reform the bonds in the hair, resulting in a permanent change in the hair's structure. Perms can be done on both short and long hair and can range from tight curls to loose waves.

Permed hair became popular in the 1980s and 1990s when big, voluminous curls were all the rage. However, many people who have permed hair eventually decide to go back to their natural hair for various reasons, such as damage from the chemicals, the desire for a change, or the need for a low-maintenance hairstyle.
