What is the Bob Haircut?

The bob haircut is a classic hairstyle that is characterized by its short length and even cut. It is typically worn straight and falls somewhere between the chin and shoulders. The bob can be styled in a variety of ways, including with or without bangs, and can be adapted to suit any face shape or hair type.

The History and Myth of the Bob Haircut

The bob haircut first gained popularity in the 1920s, when women began to rebel against traditional gender roles and embrace a more liberated lifestyle. The bob was seen as a symbol of women's independence and freedom, and it quickly became a popular choice among the fashionable elite.

There is a common myth that the bob haircut was created by a famous hairdresser named Vidal Sassoon in the 1960s. While Sassoon did popularize the bob during this time, the hairstyle actually dates back much further. The bob can be traced back to ancient Egypt, where it was worn by both men and women.
