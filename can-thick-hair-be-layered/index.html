What Is Layering?

Layering is a haircutting technique that involves cutting different lengths of hair throughout your mane. It is often used to reduce bulk, add movement, and create a more balanced and textured look. Layering can be done on all hair types, including thick hair.

When layering thick hair, the hairstylist will strategically cut shorter layers around the face and longer layers throughout the rest of the hair. This helps thin out the hair and add texture, making it more manageable and easier to style. Layering can also help create a more balanced and harmonious look by adding dimension and movement to your hair.

The History and Myth of Layering Thick Hair

Layering thick hair has been a popular technique for decades. It first gained popularity in the 1970s with the rise of the shag haircut, which featured heavily layered hair. Since then, layering has remained a popular choice for those with thick hair who want to add movement and texture to their locks.

There is a common myth that layering thick hair will make it look thinner. While it is true that layering can reduce bulk, it does not necessarily make your hair look thinner. Instead, layering adds texture and dimension to your hair, making it look more stylish and modern. The key is to work with a skilled hairstylist who can create the right layers for your hair type and desired style.
