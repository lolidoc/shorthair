Understanding Hair Loss in 65-Year-Old Females

As a 65-year-old female, you may be wondering why hair loss is occurring at this stage of your life. Hair loss in women is often attributed to hormonal changes, specifically the decrease in estrogen levels that occurs during menopause. Estrogen helps to promote hair growth, so when levels decline, hair may become thinner and more prone to shedding.

In addition to hormonal changes, genetics can also play a role in hair loss. If your mother or other female relatives experienced thinning hair or hair loss, you may be more likely to experience it as well. This is known as female pattern hair loss, and it typically presents as a gradual thinning of the hair on the top of the scalp.

Other factors, such as certain medical conditions or medications, can also contribute to hair loss in women. Conditions like thyroid disorders, autoimmune diseases, and nutritional deficiencies can all impact the health of your hair. Medications like chemotherapy drugs or certain blood pressure medications may also cause hair thinning and loss.
