What Was the Style in the 60s

The Swinging Sixties

The 1960s, often referred to as the "Swinging Sixties", was a decade of significant cultural and social change. This era witnessed a revolution in fashion, music, art, and lifestyle. The style of the 60s was influenced by various movements, including the hippie counterculture, mod fashion, and the British Invasion.

Hippie Fashion

One of the most iconic styles of the 60s was the hippie fashion. Hippies rejected mainstream norms and embraced a bohemian lifestyle. Their fashion was characterized by loose, flowing garments, bell-bottom pants, tie-dye prints, and vibrant colors. They often adorned themselves with accessories like headbands, beads, and peace signs.

Mod Fashion

Another influential style of the 60s was mod fashion. Mod culture originated in London and quickly spread across the globe. It was characterized by bold, geometric patterns, mini skirts, and sharp, tailored suits. Mod fashion embraced a futuristic and youthful aesthetic, with models like Twiggy becoming the epitome of the mod look.

The British Invasion

The 60s also saw the rise of the British Invasion in music, which had a significant impact on fashion. Bands like The Beatles and The Rolling Stones popularized the "mop-top" hairstyle, collarless jackets, and skinny pants. Their influence extended to the masses, and people started emulating their style.

The Influence of Pop Art

Pop art, a movement that emerged in the 1950s, continued to influence the style of the 60s. Artists like Andy Warhol and Roy Lichtenstein drew inspiration from popular culture and consumerism. Their vibrant and bold artworks translated into fashion, with clothing featuring pop art prints and bright colors.

Makeup and Hairstyles

The 60s witnessed a departure from the conservative hairstyles and makeup of the previous decades. Women embraced more daring looks, such as the beehive hairstyle, bouffant hairdos, and the pixie cut. Makeup became more experimental, with heavy eyeliner, false lashes, and bright, statement lips being popular choices.

Conclusion

The style of the 60s was a blend of various influences, reflecting the cultural and social changes of the time. From the hippie fashion to mod culture and the British Invasion, this era left a lasting impact on fashion and continues to inspire designers and individuals today. The 60s were a time of freedom, self-expression, and breaking traditional norms, resulting in an iconic and revolutionary style.
