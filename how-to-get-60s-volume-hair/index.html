Are you tired of flat, lifeless hair? Do you want to achieve the iconic volume of 60s hairstyles? If so, you've come to the right place! In this blog post, we'll share tips and tricks on how to get 60s volume hair and bring back the retro glamour.

Many people struggle with achieving volume in their hair, especially when trying to recreate the iconic styles of the 60s. Flat, limp hair can be frustrating and make you feel less confident. But don't worry, we have some solutions for you.

To get 60s volume hair, you need to start with a good foundation. It's important to use the right products and styling techniques to create the desired volume. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Start with a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner is key to achieving 60s volume hair. Look for products that are specifically designed to add volume and lift to your hair. These products typically contain ingredients that create texture and body.

When washing your hair, focus on the roots and massage the shampoo into your scalp to stimulate blood flow and promote volume. Rinse thoroughly and follow with a volumizing conditioner, focusing on the ends of your hair to avoid weighing down the roots.

Step 2: Apply a Volumizing Mousse or Spray

After washing and towel-drying your hair, apply a volumizing mousse or spray to the roots. These products work by adding lift and texture to your hair, giving it that 60s volume. Apply the product evenly and comb through your hair to distribute it.

For added volume, flip your head upside down and blow-dry your hair, focusing on the roots. This will help create lift and body. Once your hair is dry, flip it back and use a round brush to further enhance the volume.

Step 3: Tease and Backcomb

To achieve maximum volume, you can tease or backcomb your hair. This technique involves gently combing the hair backward towards the roots, creating a cushion of volume. Start by sectioning your hair and backcomb each section from the mid-lengths to the roots.

Be gentle when teasing your hair to avoid causing damage. Once you've teased all the desired sections, use a brush or comb to smooth the top layer and create a polished look while keeping the volume intact.

Step 4: Set with Hairspray

To ensure your 60s volume hair stays in place all day, finish off with a generous amount of hairspray. Choose a hairspray that provides strong hold without weighing down your hair. Spray it evenly over your styled hair, focusing on the roots for extra lift.

Conclusion of how to get 60s volume hair

Achieving 60s volume hair may require a bit of effort and the right products, but the results are worth it. Follow these steps and enjoy the retro glamour of voluminous, bouncy hair. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be discouraged if it takes a few tries to get it right. Have fun experimenting with different styles and unleash your inner 60s icon!
