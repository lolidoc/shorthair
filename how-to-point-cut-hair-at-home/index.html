What is Point Cutting?

Point cutting is a hair cutting technique that involves making small, upward cuts into the hair to create texture and movement. This technique is commonly used to thin out thick hair, add layers, or create a more natural-looking haircut. Point cutting is different from straight cutting, as it creates a softer and more blended look.

History and Myth of Point Cutting

The history of point cutting can be traced back to the 1970s when hairstylists began experimenting with different cutting techniques to create unique and edgy hairstyles. Point cutting became popular as it allowed hairstylists to add texture and movement to the hair, creating a more natural and effortless look. However, there is no myth associated with point cutting.

The Hidden Secret of Point Cutting

The hidden secret of point cutting is that it allows you to personalize your haircut to suit your individual style and preferences. By making small, upward cuts into the hair, you can create a more textured and layered look that adds volume and movement. Point cutting also helps to remove bulk from thick hair, making it easier to style and manage.

Recommendation for Point Cutting at Home

If you are new to point cutting, it is recommended to start with small, conservative cuts and gradually work your way up to more dramatic cuts. It is also important to invest in a good pair of scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Sharp scissors will make the cutting process easier and help you achieve cleaner, more precise cuts.

Point Cutting Techniques and Tips

1. Start with clean, towel-dried hair to ensure that it is easy to work with.

2. Use a comb to remove any tangles or knots before starting the point cutting process.

3. Take small sections of hair between your index and middle fingers and hold them away from your scalp.

4. Make small, upward cuts into the hair using sharp scissors, pointing them towards the ends of the hair.

5. Work your way around your head, cutting small sections of hair at a time, until you achieve your desired look.

6. Remember to take breaks and check your progress in the mirror to ensure that you are achieving an even and balanced haircut.

Common Questions About Point Cutting

1. How often should I point cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and desired style. Some people may need to point cut their hair every 4-6 weeks, while others may only need to do it every 8-12 weeks.

2. Can I point cut my own hair if it is curly?

Yes, point cutting can be done on curly hair. However, it is important to be cautious and start with small cuts to avoid creating uneven layers.

3. Can point cutting be done on short hair?

Yes, point cutting can be done on short hair. However, it may be more challenging to achieve the desired look, and it is recommended to seek professional help if you are unsure.

4. What if I make a mistake while point cutting?

If you make a mistake while point cutting, don't panic. Hair grows back, and you can always seek professional help to fix any issues.
