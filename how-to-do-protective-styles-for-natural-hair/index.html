How To Do Protective Styles For Natural Hair

Introduction

If you have natural hair, you know how important it is to protect it from damage and breakage. One of the best ways to do this is by incorporating protective styles into your hair care routine. Protective styles not only keep your hair safe but also allow it to grow and thrive. In this article, we will guide you through the process of achieving beautiful and effective protective styles for your natural hair.

1. Prepping Your Hair

Before diving into any protective style, it is crucial to prep your hair properly. Start by washing and conditioning your hair using products that suit your hair type. Moisturize your hair with a leave-in conditioner and apply a heat protectant if you plan to use heat styling tools.

2. Choosing the Right Style

There are numerous protective styles available for natural hair, including braids, twists, buns, and wigs. Consider factors such as your hair length, texture, and personal preference when deciding which style to choose. It is essential to select a style that will not put excessive tension on your scalp or hair strands.

3. Proper Sectioning

Divide your hair into manageable sections before starting any protective style. This will make the process easier and ensure that each section receives proper attention. Use clips or hair ties to secure the sections you are not currently working on.

4. Moisturize and Seal

Before installing any protective style, make sure your hair is adequately moisturized. Apply a water-based moisturizer or leave-in conditioner to each section of hair. Follow up with an oil or butter-based sealant to lock in the moisture. This will prevent your hair from drying out and becoming brittle.

5. Installing Braids or Twists

If you opt for braids or twists, start by parting a small section of hair at the root. Apply a styling product such as gel or cream to the section and begin braiding or twisting from the root to the ends. Make sure to be gentle and avoid pulling too tightly to prevent hair breakage.

6. Creating a Bun

To achieve a protective bun, gather your hair into a high or low ponytail. Twist or braid the ponytail and wrap it around its base, securing it with bobby pins. This style keeps your ends tucked away and protects them from environmental damage.

7. Wearing a Wig

Wigs are an excellent option for protective styling as they provide a barrier between your natural hair and external elements. Before wearing a wig, ensure your hair is properly moisturized and protected with a wig cap. Style the wig as desired, and you're good to go!

8. Nighttime Care

Protective styles should also be maintained during sleep. Wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf or wear a satin bonnet before going to bed. This will prevent friction and keep your style intact.

9. Routine Maintenance

While protective styles can last for weeks, it is essential to care for your hair underneath. Moisturize your scalp and roots with a lightweight oil or moisturizing spray. Cleanse your scalp as needed to prevent product buildup and maintain a healthy environment for hair growth.

10. Taking Down the Style

When it's time to remove your protective style, be gentle to avoid unnecessary breakage. Use a detangling spray or conditioner to soften your hair before unraveling braids or twists. Take your time and be patient during the takedown process.

Conclusion

Protective styles are a fantastic way to maintain and protect your natural hair. By following these steps and incorporating these styles into your hair care routine, you can promote hair growth and keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
