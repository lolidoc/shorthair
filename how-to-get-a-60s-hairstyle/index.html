Introduction

The 60s was an iconic era for fashion and hairstyles, characterized by its bold and glamorous looks. If you want to channel the retro vibes and rock a 60s hairstyle, you've come to the right place. In this article, we will guide you through the steps to achieve a stunning 60s-inspired hairdo that will make heads turn.

1. Voluminous Beehive

The beehive hairstyle was one of the most popular looks in the 60s. To achieve this voluminous 'do, start by backcombing your hair at the crown to create height. Then, gather your hair into a high ponytail and tease it to add more volume. Finally, wrap the ponytail around its base and secure it with bobby pins, creating a beehive shape.

2. Sleek and Straight

If you prefer a more understated 60s look, go for the sleek and straight hairstyle. Start by applying a heat protectant spray to your hair. Then, use a flat iron to straighten your locks, section by section. Finish off with a shine serum for that extra polished and glossy finish.

3. Big and Bouncy Curls

Curls were a staple in 60s hairstyles. To achieve big and bouncy curls, start by applying a curl-enhancing mousse to damp hair. Then, use a large-barrel curling iron to curl small sections of your hair. Once all the curls are done, gently brush them out with a wide-toothed comb for a softer and more voluminous look.

4. Twiggy-Inspired Pixie Cut

If you're feeling adventurous, why not try a Twiggy-inspired pixie cut? This iconic 60s hairstyle is all about short, choppy layers and a super sleek finish. Visit a professional hairstylist who specializes in pixie cuts to achieve the perfect Twiggy look that suits your face shape.

5. Glamorous Victory Rolls

Victory rolls are a classic 60s hairstyle that exudes glamour and sophistication. Start by sectioning off a small portion of hair at the front. Then, take a fine-toothed comb and backcomb the section to create volume. Next, roll the section towards your scalp and secure it with bobby pins. Repeat the process on the other side, and you'll have stunning victory rolls.

6. Elegant French Twist

The French twist is a timeless hairstyle that was popularized in the 60s. To achieve this elegant look, start by combing your hair back and gathering it into a low ponytail. Twist the ponytail upwards, tucking and pinning it against your head. Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands, and finish off with a hairspray for extra hold.

7. Hippie-Inspired Braids

The 60s was also the era of hippie fashion, and braided hairstyles were a common sight. To create a hippie-inspired braided look, part your hair down the middle and create two loose braids on each side. Pull out a few strands of hair to frame your face and give it that effortless bohemian vibe.

8. Retro Headscarf

A retro headscarf is a quick and easy way to add a 60s touch to any hairstyle. Simply tie a colorful or patterned scarf around your head, making sure to cover your hairline. You can leave your hair down or style it in a bun or ponytail for a chic and retro look.

9. Teased Half-Up Bouffant

The half-up bouffant is a versatile and classy 60s hairstyle that works for various hair lengths. Start by backcombing the crown section of your hair to create volume. Then, gather the teased section and secure it with bobby pins at the back of your head. Leave the rest of your hair down or style it in loose waves for an elegant finish.

10. Statement Hair Accessories

Complete your 60s hairstyle with statement hair accessories such as oversized bows, headbands, or embellished clips. These accessories were a popular trend in the 60s and can instantly elevate your look, adding that extra touch of retro glamour.

Conclusion

With these tips and techniques, you can easily achieve a stunning 60s hairstyle that brings back the glamour and nostalgia of the iconic era. Whether you opt for a beehive, sleek straight locks, or braids, embrace your inner retro queen and rock that 60s look with confidence!
