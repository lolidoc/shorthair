What is 1960s Hair?

1960s hair refers to the various hairstyles that were popular during the 1960s. This decade saw a wide range of styles, from the sleek and polished looks of the early 60s to the more natural and free-spirited styles of the late 60s. Some iconic hairstyles from this era include the beehive, the bouffant, the mod bob, and the long, loose waves.

One of the key characteristics of 1960s hair is volume. Whether it's achieved through teasing, backcombing, or the use of padding, voluminous hair was a must during this time. Additionally, 1960s hair often featured bold accessories such as headbands, scarves, and oversized bows.

The History and Myth of 1960s Hair

The 1960s was a time of great social and cultural change, and this was reflected in the hairstyles of the era. In the early 60s, women were still adhering to the more polished and conservative beauty standards of the 1950s. However, as the decade progressed, the influence of the youth culture and the rise of the counterculture movement led to a shift towards more natural and unconventional styles.

One of the most iconic hairstyles of the 1960s is the beehive. This towering hairstyle was popularized by celebrities such as Audrey Hepburn and Jackie Kennedy and became a symbol of sophistication and glamour. The beehive was achieved by backcombing the hair to create volume and then shaping it into a high, rounded shape.

The Hidden Secret of 1960s Hair

The key to achieving authentic 1960s hair lies in the art of teasing. Teasing, also known as backcombing or ratting, involves combing the hair against its natural direction to create volume and texture. This technique was widely used in the 1960s to achieve the iconic bouffant and beehive hairstyles.

To tease your hair, start by sectioning off a small portion at the crown of your head. Hold this section straight up, and using a teasing comb or brush, gently comb the hair downwards towards your scalp. Repeat this process several times, moving from section to section, until you have achieved the desired level of volume. To smooth out the top layer of hair, gently brush over it with a soft bristle brush.
