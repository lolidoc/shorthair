Are you curious about what styles were popular in the 60s? The 1960s were a time of cultural revolution and self-expression, and this was reflected in the fashion choices of the era. From the iconic mod look to the bohemian hippie style, the 60s were a decade of bold and daring fashion statements. In this blog post, we will explore the popular styles of the 60s and dive deeper into what made them so influential.

The 60s were a time of change, and fashion was no exception. The youth of the era were eager to break away from the conservative styles of the past and embrace a more rebellious and individualistic approach to dressing. This desire for self-expression led to the rise of various fashion movements that continue to inspire designers and fashionistas today.

One of the most iconic styles of the 60s was the mod look. This style was influenced by the mod subculture, which originated in London and quickly spread across the globe. Mod fashion was characterized by clean lines, bold geometric patterns, and a monochromatic color palette. Mini skirts, fitted dresses, and tailored suits were popular choices for mod fashion enthusiasts.

Another popular style of the 60s was the bohemian hippie look. This style was heavily influenced by the counterculture movement of the era and was associated with peace, love, and non-conformity. Flowy maxi dresses, bell-bottom pants, fringe details, and tie-dye prints were all key elements of the bohemian hippie style.

The History and Myth of 60s Fashion

The 60s were a time of great social and political change, and this was reflected in the fashion of the era. Fashion became a way for individuals to express their political beliefs, challenge societal norms, and embrace their own unique identities. The mod look and bohemian hippie style were both born out of this desire for self-expression and rebellion.

Mod fashion originated in London in the late 1950s and early 1960s. It was a reaction against the conservative styles of the post-war era and a celebration of youth culture. The mod look was characterized by its clean lines, bold colors, and futuristic aesthetic. It was a style that embraced modernity and embraced the idea of youth as a cultural force.

The bohemian hippie style, on the other hand, was a reaction against the consumerism and materialism of the mainstream culture. It was a style that celebrated nature, peace, and non-conformity. The hippie movement was influenced by various subcultures, including the beatniks and the folk revival movement, and it embraced a more relaxed and free-spirited approach to fashion.

The Hidden Secrets of 60s Fashion

One of the hidden secrets of 60s fashion is the influence of the space race on the mod look. The space race between the United States and the Soviet Union was a major cultural event of the era, and it had a significant impact on popular culture, including fashion. The mod look was heavily influenced by the futuristic aesthetic of the space age, with designers incorporating futuristic materials and silhouettes into their designs.

Another hidden secret of 60s fashion is the influence of African and Asian cultures on the bohemian hippie style. The counterculture movement of the era was characterized by its interest in global cultures and its rejection of Western imperialism. This interest in global cultures was reflected in the fashion choices of the era, with designers incorporating elements of African and Asian textiles, prints, and silhouettes into their designs.

Recommendations for 60s Fashion

If you're looking to incorporate 60s fashion into your wardrobe, there are several key pieces and styling tips to keep in mind. For the mod look, opt for clean lines, bold colors, and geometric prints. Mini skirts, shift dresses, and tailored suits are all classic mod pieces that can be easily incorporated into a modern wardrobe.

For the bohemian hippie style, embrace flowy silhouettes, vibrant prints, and natural fabrics. Maxi dresses, bell-bottom pants, and fringe details are all key elements of this style. Don't be afraid to mix and match different patterns and textures to create a unique and bohemian-inspired look.

More about 60s Fashion

If you're interested in learning more about 60s fashion, there are several resources available that can provide further insight into the era. Books such as "The 60s: The Story of a Decade" by Jann S. Wenner and "Sixties Fashion: From 'Less is More' to Youthquake" by Jonathan Walford offer a comprehensive look at the fashion trends and cultural movements of the era.

Tips for Embracing 60s Fashion

If you're looking to embrace 60s fashion in your own wardrobe, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  1. Start with key pieces: Incorporate classic 60s pieces, such as mini skirts, shift dresses, and bell-bottom pants, into your wardrobe.
  2. Play with color and pattern: Don't be afraid to experiment with bold colors and vibrant prints, such as geometric patterns and tie-dye.
  3. Accessorize with statement pieces: Add the finishing touch to your 60s-inspired look with statement accessories, such as oversized sunglasses, statement jewelry, and retro-inspired handbags.
  4. Have fun and be creative: The 60s were all about self-expression and individuality, so don't be afraid to have fun and experiment with different styles and looks.

Fun Facts about 60s Fashion

Did you know that the mini skirt, one of the iconic fashion pieces of the 60s, was popularized by designer Mary Quant? Quant is credited with popularizing the mini skirt and making it a staple of mod fashion.

Another fun fact is that the Beatles, one of the most influential bands of the era, played a significant role in popularizing mod fashion. The band's members, especially Paul McCartney and John Lennon, were known for their mod-inspired style, which heavily influenced their fans and fashion trends of the era.

How to Incorporate 60s Fashion into Your Wardrobe

If you're looking to incorporate 60s fashion into your wardrobe, start by finding inspiration from the era. Look for vintage photos, movies, and magazines from the 60s to get a sense of the fashion trends and styles of the era. Once you have a sense of the overall aesthetic, start by incorporating key pieces into your wardrobe, such as mini skirts, shift dresses, and bell-bottom pants. Don't be afraid to experiment with bold colors and vibrant prints, and have fun with accessories, such as oversized sunglasses and statement jewelry.

What If You're Not a Fan of 60s Fashion?

If you're not a fan of 60s fashion, that's totally okay! Fashion is all about personal style and self-expression, so it's important to wear what makes you feel confident and comfortable. There are plenty of other fashion eras and styles to explore, so don't be afraid to experiment and find what works best for you.
